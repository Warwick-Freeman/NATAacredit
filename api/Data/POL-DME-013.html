MEDICAL CPR/AED EMERGENCY DRILL

1. Purpose

To ensure availability and readiness of appropriate medical emergency equipment in the event of required use. To provide staff with the training necessary to respond to a cardiopulmonary medical emergency.

2. Policy

It is the policy that appropriate emergency medical equipment will be available and accessible to all staff in the event of a cardiopulmonary event and maintained in accordance with manufacturer’s recommendations. All personnel will take part in an annual cardiopulmonary drill using an automated external device (AED). If all personnel cannot attend the drill, more than one drill may need to be arranged. Attendance will be recorded, and a copy will be maintained in the individual the Nexus 360 Staff & Training module.

3. Procedure

Equipment used in an emergency (AED, barrier for resuscitation, oxygen) is located in the ___Nexus 360__office.

All staff will be required to be trained in the use of emergency equipment annually (AED). This will include conducting an annual cardiopulmonary drill.

Plan the emergency drill:

Schedule a date and time for the practice drill.

Key individuals responsible for the coordination/facilitation of the drill must be assigned.

Facilitator identifies all the players in the drill.

Assign staff to their various responsibilities for purposes of the drill:

Staff member #1 is assigned to evaluation room 1-2.

Staff member #2 assigned to evaluation room, front desk and waiting room (as applicable).

One individual observer places a manikin at the designated time in the designated location.

One individual is responsible for setting the clock and recording.

Determine the scenario that will be used for the drill.

Option 1—During a scheduled follow-up face-to-face visit, upon patient escorted being placed in the evaluation room, the adult becomes unresponsive, vital signs absent, patient is in cardiac arrest.

Option 2—Patient in the waiting room, collapsed on the floor.

Notify the team that a drill may take place within a couple of weeks to allow them time to review the emergency protocols.

Do not disclose the exact date or time.

Instruct the staff to respond to the drill as if it was a real emergency.

Ensure equipment/supplies are available, including:

clothed mannikin to represent the patient.

AED

stethoscope

EKG monitor

CPR masks

oxygen

stopwatch

Perform the drill:

A manikin is placed in the designated room.

Facilitator calls out “patient unresponsive in cardiac arrest; call 911.”

The observer starts the time clock and fills out the checklist, recording times when each event occurs.

After the drill:

Discuss the drill with the sleep team to gain feedback from staff and discuss/address any concerns.

Inform the team of the evaluation process.

Evaluate the Drill

What did the team do right?

What could have been done better?

Provide a Summary of the Evaluation.

areas that went well

areas for improvement/recommendation

Schedule remediation if necessary

Decide if repeat drill will be necessary.

Determine if all other applicable emergency procedures were followed (e.g., contact procedures

access to front door for ER personnel, other patient is being monitored).

Maintenance of Emergency Equipment

Staff will check to ensure the AED light is flashing “ready” daily and document on the AED

check log the “light is on.”

An authorized clinic staff member _(insert name)_____ will inspect the expiration dates of the AED

battery and pads monthly and ensure the supply bag attached contains all necessary supplies

(CPR mask, razor, dry cloth, and scissors.)

Any reordering of supplies is to be done in advance.
