significant Adverse Event Reporting

1. Purpose

To promptly and comprehensively report significant adverse events that occur while a patient is in our care.

2. Policy

Significant adverse events will be reported and documented for investigative purposes to ensure the safety of our patients and provide improvement in the delivery of care.

3. Definitions

Significant Adverse Event: An unexpected or serious incident that occurs while a patient is on site that may impact the patient’s or staff’s health or safety. Significant adverse events are identified in the Significant Adverse Events policy (reference Network Section N-21).

4. Procedure

Any staff member who becomes aware of any significant adverse event is to report it immediately to (insert designated supervisor in authority)

The details of the event are to be documented as soon as possible after the event by the staff member who either identified or was involved in the occurrence. (Use Significant Adverse Event Reporting form) ____

Identify the significant adverse event.

Describe what happened through the collection and organization of the data/facts surrounding the event to understand what occurred.

Provide a narrative description of what happened.

A narrative description is to be completed by the employee initiating the report who was most involved in the event.

What to include in a well-written narrative description:

Who was involved in the event/situation?

When did it happen? Specify time points if possible.

Where did it happen? Specify location.

Signature and title of employee writing the narrative description.

Signature of individual receiving the form (supervisor/manager in administrative role).

Upon completing the form, route form to the site director for investigative analysis.
