1. Purpose

Completing patient management and follow-up ensures patients are provided with the highest quality clinical care through ongoing comprehensive evaluation and assessment.

2. Policy

It is the policy of (insert name of clinic) that all patients diagnosed with sleep disorders will be offered ongoing evaluation, management, and follow-up assessment. Patients prescribed positive airway pressure (PAP) treatment by the sleep medical staff will receive PAP assessment follow-up within 12 weeks of treatment initiation.

3. Procedure

All patient interactions will be documented in the patients electronic or paper medical record.

Comprehensive follow-up care for all patients with sleep disorders through scheduling regular appointments to evaluate the treatment plan and modify as appropriate.

Each patient prescribed PAP treatment by clinic medical staff will be offered a follow-up PAP assessment within 12 weeks of treatment initiation.

Patients will be contacted by telephone 8 weeks after treatment initiation. Technical staff will gather the subjective response of the patient through a telephone questionnaire.

Subjective response will be documented in the patient medical record.

Device download information will be remotely downloaded after 8 weeks of treatment and documented in the patient’s medical record.

If the patient does not respond to the telephone inquiry, clinic staff will attempt to contact the patient again by telephone and letter to complete a telephone questionnaire assessment.

If the telephone questionnaire is not completed, staff will attempt to schedule an in-person appointment with the patient.

Attempts to follow up with the patient will be documented in the patient’s medical record.

If the patient reports a positive response to therapy and the device download confirms use and response to therapy, this will be documented in the patient record.

If the patient reports a negative response regarding therapy and/or the device download shows an inadequate response, clinic staff will schedule an in-person appointment to discuss the response to therapy, which will include but not be limited to:

assessment of use of the device

assessment of intolerance or nonacceptance of the device

review of the device download

review of telephone questionnaire response/previous subjective response

review of device patient interface

addressing other underlying causes of inadequate response

For clinics that only provide HSAT:

An existing written agreement defining the relationship for conducting in-lab diagnostic testing will be maintained in order to ensure availability when needed with (Insert AASM Accredited Name and Accreditation Number).
