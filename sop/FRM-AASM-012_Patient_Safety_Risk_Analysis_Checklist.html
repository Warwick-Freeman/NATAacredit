External elements Risk level** Comments
Surface of sidewalks and walkways to lab are level, no need of repair.
Parking lots are well lit.
Entry signs to lab clearly marked, visible, and well lit.
A security system is in place to prevent unauthorized access.
Entrance/doors to lab are kept locked.
Internal elements
Fire protection
Exits are unobstructed and accessible for emergency personnel.
Exit doors are, or can be, unlocked quickly during emergency evacuations.
Exits and routes are clearly marked and are equipped with emergency lighting.
Fire and smoke detection systems have been tested.
Temporary/backup fire detection systems are inspected and tested monthly.
Automatic sprinkler system is in place.
Location of fire extinguishers is easily found.
Staff have all been trained on use of extinguishers.
Any flammable housekeeping materials are kept away from source of combustion and secured in closed and vented cabinet.
Fire inspection by local fire department conducted.
General maintenance-structural-safety
Sleep testing rooms and offices are cleaned and maintained to prevent injury or falls.
Floor and stair surfaces are clean and dry, not slippery.
Equipment cleaning areas are separate from dirty equipment and food handling locations.
Storage areas are kept free from any pest hazards.
Sleep facility/office is free from infestations.
Floors are free from loose boards, rugs, holes, electrical cords, etc.
Personal items of patients are maintained appropriately to prevent theft.
Cellular phones available and in working order
Extension cords are out of aisles and well secured.
Shower/bathroom floors are well maintained, with nonslippery floors.
Adequate clearance is allowed in halls and aisles.
Cameras are in place for video monitoring to prevent assault or intrusion in halls and sleep rooms.
Electrical outlets are covered in rooms when not in use (pediatric sites).
Uneven surfaces are easily identifiable.
Infection control
Potentially hazardous materials are kept in locked cabinet and clearly labeled.
Safety data sheets are maintained for easy access (SDS).
Hazardous material is stored below eye level.
Oxygen equipment is stored properly.
Biohazard waste containers are stored in locked area.
Personal protective equipment accessible: masks, gloves, gowns, handwashing equipment
Equipment is cleaned according to manufacturer’s recommendations with appropriate cleaning supplies.
Other uneven surfaces are easily identifiable.
