1. Purpose ASA 5.3.4.2 / 5.5.5.3(a)

To verify, prior to each in-lab or ambulatory sleep study, that the polysomnographic recording system, sensors and associated channels are correctly applied and producing physiologically valid signals in accordance with the AASM Manual for the Scoring of Sleep and Associated Events (v2.5+) and the ASA Guidelines for Sleep Studies in Adults / ASTA-ASA Paediatric Addendum. The biocalibration ("biocal") is the patient-performed component of the equipment verification programme (ASA cl. 5.3.4).

2. Scope

Applies to all attended Type 1 polysomnography (PSG), CPAP/PAP titration, MSLT/MWT, and unattended Type 2 home studies performed by the service in adult and paediatric patients. Where a patient is unable to cooperate (eg infant, severe cognitive impairment), the alternative qualitative verification described in Section 7 applies.

3. Responsibilities

RoleResponsibility
Recording TechnologistPerforms biocal, annotates record, judges acceptability, performs corrective re-application.
Senior Scientist / TechnologistReviews biocal records for trends, signs off out-of-spec exceptions, authorises study commencement when criteria not met.
Reporting PhysicianReviews biocal as part of raw-data review (ASA 5.8.2) and reports any signal quality limitations.
Quality ManagerAudits biocal completion rate as a quality indicator (ASA 5.6.3).

4. Definitions

  • Biocalibration (biocal): a sequence of patient-performed physiological manoeuvres used to confirm signal polarity, amplitude, and channel labelling.
  • Impedance check: objective electrical measurement of electrode-to-skin contact resistance.
  • AASM Manual: Berry RB et al, The AASM Manual for the Scoring of Sleep and Associated Events, current version.

5. Equipment & Materials

  • PSG acquisition system (ARTG-listed, current verification certificate within calibration interval - ASA 5.3).
  • Electrodes / sensors per montage: EEG (F3-M2, F4-M1, C3-M2, C4-M1, O1-M2, O2-M1), EOG (E1-M2, E2-M2), chin EMG (3 electrodes), ECG, right & left anterior tibialis EMG, oronasal thermal sensor, nasal pressure transducer, RIP thoracic and abdominal belts, SpO2 probe with pulse waveform, snore sensor, body-position sensor, optional CO2 sensor, optional PAP-flow channel.
  • Time-synchronised infrared video (mandatory paediatric, recommended adult).
  • Skin-prep abrasive, conductive paste, tape, measuring tape.
  • Recording sheet / electronic biocal worksheet.

6. Pre-Biocalibration Checks

  1. Confirm patient identity using three identifiers (ASA 5.4.4).
  2. Confirm referral, study type and any special instructions (eg split-night, paediatric NIV).
  3. Apply electrodes per the International 10-20 system and ASA/AASM montage; head measurement, skin preparation, and impedance verified at <5 kΩ for EEG/EOG/EMG (target <10 kΩ for limb EMG).
  4. Verify channel labelling and display sensitivity defaults per AASM technical specifications (EEG 7 µV/mm, EOG 7 µV/mm, chin EMG 7 µV/mm, ECG 1 mV calibration, respiratory channels per manufacturer).
  5. Confirm low/high-frequency filters per AASM defaults (EEG/EOG 0.3-35 Hz; chin & leg EMG 10-100 Hz; ECG 0.3-70 Hz; airflow 0.1-15 Hz).
  6. Run system impedance/calibration self-test; record result in the patient file.

7. Biocalibration Procedure AASM Manual / ASA 5.3.4.2

Perform every step in the order shown. For each step, instruct the patient clearly, annotate the start of the manoeuvre on the recording, observe at least the channels listed under Channels to verify, and judge against the acceptance criteria. Each manoeuvre should be held / repeated for the duration shown.

# Instruction to patient Duration Channels to verify Expected signal
1 "Lie still, keep eyes open and look straight ahead at a fixed point." 30 s EEG, EOG, chin EMG Low-amplitude mixed-frequency EEG; suppressed alpha; flat EOG.
2 "Close your eyes and stay still." 30 s EEG (O1, O2), EOG Posterior alpha rhythm (8-13 Hz) clearly present over occipital derivations.
3 "Eyes closed - look up and down, up and down." Repeat 5 times. ~10 s EOG (E1, E2) In-phase vertical deflections in both EOG channels (eyes positive going up).
4 "Eyes closed - look left and right, left and right." Repeat 5 times. ~10 s EOG (E1, E2) Out-of-phase deflections - E1 and E2 mirror each other, confirming polarity and that left/right channels are not swapped.
5 "Blink five times slowly." ~10 s EOG, frontal EEG Five sharp, symmetric, in-phase blink artefacts in both EOG channels.
6 "Grit your teeth (clench your jaw) for 5 seconds, then relax." 5 s + relax Chin EMG Marked sustained increase in chin EMG amplitude; returns to baseline on relaxation.
7 "Hum or make a snoring sound." ~5 s Snore sensor, microphone, airflow Clear oscillation on snore channel and microphone trace.
8 "Take a deep breath in through your nose, then out through your nose." Repeat twice. ~15 s Nasal pressure, oronasal thermal, thorax/abdomen RIP Strong inspiratory deflection on nasal pressure and thermal; in-phase chest and abdominal excursion.
9 "Close your mouth - breathe only through your mouth (pinch nose). Then breathe only through your nose." ~15 s Nasal pressure, oronasal thermal Nasal pressure flattens during mouth breathing; thermal signal persists - confirms each pathway independently.
10 "Take a deep breath in and hold it for 10 seconds. Then breathe out." 10 s RIP belts, airflow, SpO2 Flat airflow with stable chest/abdominal position during hold; SpO2 waveform remains; transient O2 change after release.
11 "Simulate snoring or paradoxical breathing (chest in, abdomen out)." Optional - omit for paediatric. ~10 s Thorax / abdomen RIP Visible paradox - thoracic and abdominal traces move out of phase.
12 "Flex your right foot up towards your head - hold 2 seconds - relax." Repeat 3 times. ~10 s Right anterior tibialis EMG Clear EMG burst on right channel only.
13 "Now flex your left foot - hold - relax." Repeat 3 times. ~10 s Left anterior tibialis EMG Clear EMG burst on left channel only - confirms no left/right swap.
14 "Roll onto your right side - back to centre - onto your left side - back to centre." ~30 s Body position sensor, RIP belts, ECG Position channel updates to each posture; respiratory and ECG remain interpretable.
15 PAP titration studies only: Fit interface, deliver 4 cmH2O, ask patient to breathe normally for 30 s. 30 s PAP flow, mask pressure, leak PAP pressure channel reads ~4 cmH2O, mask leak within manufacturer threshold.
Paediatric adaptation (ASA 5.3.4.3): for infants and children unable to follow commands, perform a qualitative verification at the bedside: confirm all channels display physiological signals during quiet wakefulness, observe spontaneous eye movements, gentle limb movement on stimulation, and clear respiratory waveforms. Document the limitation and the alternative steps performed in the patient file.

8. Acceptance Criteria

SignalAcceptance threshold
EEG / EOG / chin EMG impedance< 5 kΩ (re-prep if > 5 kΩ)
Inter-electrode impedance matchWithin 25% across derivations
EOG polarityStep 4 out-of-phase deflections clearly visible
Chin EMG>= 3x baseline amplitude on jaw clench
Leg EMGClear lateralised burst > baseline x 4
Airflow channelsVisible flow during nasal-only and mouth-only breathing, flat during breath-hold
RIP beltsSymmetrical, in-phase deflection during quiet breathing
SpO2Stable plethysmograph, signal-quality index acceptable per device manual
ECGClear QRS, no 50 Hz mains contamination
Body positionAll four positions register correctly

9. Troubleshooting

ObservationLikely causeCorrective action
High impedance / driftPoor skin prep, dried paste, loose electrodeRe-prep skin, re-apply paste, re-tape; repeat biocal.
EOG in-phase when out-of-phase expectedE1/E2 swap or reference errorVerify cable, re-label channel, repeat step 4.
Absent EMG burst on jaw clenchLoose chin electrode or reversed pairRe-apply chin electrodes; repeat step 6.
Flat nasal pressure with intact thermalBlocked nasal cannula or disconnectInspect cannula, clear / replace; re-verify step 8.
Paradoxical RIP signal during quiet breathingBelts swapped or upside-downRe-fit belts; confirm step 8.
50 Hz interferenceMains coupling, faulty earth, mobile device nearbyCheck ground, remove device, re-route cables.
SpO2 drop-outProbe placement, cold extremityReposition probe, warm extremity.
Persistent failureSuspected equipment faultWithdraw equipment from use (ASA 5.3.5(c)), log adverse equipment event, switch to verified spare.

10. Quality Assurance ASA 5.5.5.3(f), 5.6.3

  • Biocal completion is mandatory and forms a quality indicator (target 100%, threshold >= 98%/month).
  • Biocal failures that delay or abort a study are recorded as nonconformances (ASA 4.9) and trended quarterly.
  • Senior Scientist/Technologist reviews 10% of biocals each month for completeness and signal quality.
  • Trends suggesting equipment drift trigger an out-of-cycle verification per the Equipment Verification SOP.

11. Records ASA 4.13, 5.3.4.2

  • Biocal worksheet (paper or electronic) attached to the patient study file, including: study ID, three patient identifiers, date, time, technologist ID, impedance values, step-by-step pass/fail or signal-quality grading, deviations, corrective actions.
  • Retention: with the polysomnography record per the service Records Retention Policy.
  • Corrections per ASA 4.13.2.7 (cross-out, initial, date - no destructive edits).
