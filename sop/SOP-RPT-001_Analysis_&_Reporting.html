1. Purpose ASA 5.8

To produce sleep study reports that are accurate, signed, traceable and consistent with current ASA and AASM standards.

2. Scoring

  1. Performed by technologists trained and competent (paediatric studies require paediatric competence).
  2. Conducted per the AASM Manual (current version) and ASA Guidelines / paediatric addendum.
  3. Analysis time per study respected: adult >=2 h; paediatric >=4 h; Type 2 home >=1.5 h.
  4. Technologist observations recorded.

3. Physician Review

  1. Sleep physician reviews raw data epoch by epoch.
  2. Technologist scoring and observations available; feedback to scorer recorded where issues identified.
  3. Computer-aided analysis is an aid only; final analysis and interpretation are manual and by the responsible physician.
  4. Normal reference values with uncertainty estimates available.

4. Report Content (mandatory)

  • Service identification.
  • Site where the study was performed.
  • Patient identification (3-ID).
  • Date of study and date of final report.
  • Clinical / technical observations about the conduct of the study.
  • Study results plus interpretive summary signed by interpreting sleep physician.
  • Page x of y on every page.
  • Consistency with current ASA / AASM documents.

5. Preliminary & Final Reports

Preliminary reports authorised by the reporting physician and clearly status-flagged; final report cross-references the preliminary; status visible on the document.

6. Turnaround

Correspondence and PSG reports completed within 10 business days following patient contact (cl. 5.8.1).

7. Provisional CPAP Prescription Reconciliation

Where FRM-017 has been issued, the final pressure is reconciled in the final report and any change communicated to the patient and CPAP provider.

8. References

ASA Standard cl. 5.8.1 - 5.8.10; AASM Manual; ASA Guidelines.
