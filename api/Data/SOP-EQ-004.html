1. Purpose ASA 5.3.6

To ensure adverse incidents attributable to equipment are investigated and reported to the manufacturer and the relevant regulator.

ℹ️ Nexus 360 Integration: Steps in this procedure that are performed or supported by the Nexus 360 quality management system are identified with [Nexus 360] throughout this document. Staff must access Nexus 360 at http://localhost:5173 to complete these steps. The system audit trail satisfies record-keeping requirements for all digitally recorded activities.

2. Definition

Any event involving service equipment that caused, contributed to, or had the potential to cause patient or staff harm, including failure of essential safety features, software malfunction or measurement error of clinical significance.

3. Procedure

  1. Event logged in Nexus 360 NC & CAPA module [Nexus 360] within 24 hours (FRM-006 may supplement where required); equipment isolated, labelled "Do Not Use" and quarantined in Nexus 360 Equipment Register [Nexus 360].
  2. Immediate clinical risk to patients assessed; affected patients identified.
  3. Investigation by Tech Lead with Medical Director oversight; root-cause analysis (SOP-QMS-009).
  4. Report submitted to manufacturer.
  5. Report submitted to TGA (Incident Reporting & Investigation Scheme) within mandatory reporting timeframe where applicable (eg sponsor / user reporting of medical device incidents).
  6. Findings tabled at management review.

4. Records

Nexus 360 NC & CAPA record [Nexus 360]; FRM-006 (supplementary where applicable); manufacturer correspondence; TGA submission reference; CAPA records maintained in Nexus 360.

5. References

ASA Standard cl. 5.3.6; TGA Medical Device Incident Reporting.
