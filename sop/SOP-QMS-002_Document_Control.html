Document Control
Creation, approval, issue, review, change and obsolescence of controlled documents

1. Purpose ASA 4.3 AASM S-4

To ensure all documents that form the management system are uniquely identified, approved before issue, current at point of use, and removed or marked when obsolete. This SOP applies under both ASA and AASM accreditation standards.

2. Scope

Applies to all internally generated and externally sourced controlled documents including policies, SOPs, protocols, forms, equipment manuals, templates, calibration records and educational guides, in any media (paper or electronic). Under AASM accreditation, the Policy and Procedures Manual and all AASM-required policies and protocols are within scope.

3. Responsibilities

  • Quality Manager — maintains the master controlled-document register in Nexus 360; coordinates peer review scheduling; approves issue and obsolescence.
  • Document Owner — drafts and revises content; ensures accuracy and standard alignment; raises FRM-027 Document Change Request for any new or revised document.
  • Authoriser (Medical Director / Network Director / Site Director) — signs documents for issue. Under AASM accreditation the Network Director must formally review and approve all policies and protocols at least annually (AASM N-2.3).
  • Peer Reviewer — checks content, clause alignment, and consistency with related documents before the Authoriser approves.
  • All staff — use only current authorised versions accessible in Nexus 360.

4. Document Identification

Every controlled document carries: title; unique document ID (PREFIX-AREA-nnn); revision number; effective date; next review date; authoriser; and page numbering. Documents are categorised in Nexus 360 as manual, policies, SOPs, forms, or records. AASM-specific documents use the prefixes POL-NET, POL-CLI, POL-LAB, POL-HST, POL-DME, PRO-LAB, PRO-HST, or FRM-AASM and appear in AASM mode only. Cross-cutting QMS documents (SOP-QMS, SOP-HR, SOP-DATA) appear in both ASA and AASM modes.

5. Workflow

All controlled documents pass through the following five-step workflow, tracked in Nexus 360. Each step is date-stamped and attributed to the responsible person. Documents may not advance past Approval without the required role permission.

Step Responsible Action Nexus 360 permission
1. Draft Document Owner Create or revise content using the approved HTML template. Raise FRM-027 Document Change Request. Any authorised user
2. Peer review Peer Reviewer Check content, accuracy, and alignment with applicable ASA clauses and/or AASM standards. Return comments to Owner; Owner resolves and re-submits. canPeerReviewDoc
3. Approval Authoriser Confirm the document meets all requirements and approve for issue. For AASM policies, the Network Director provides approval; the annual blanket review (section 8) satisfies AASM N-2.3. canApproveDoc
4. Issue Quality Manager Assign revision number; set status to Issued in Nexus 360; update the master register (REG-001); archive the prior version; notify affected staff. canIssueDoc
5. Periodic review Document Owner Review document currency. Default cycle: 24 months (forms: 12 months). Nexus 360 alerts owners 60 days before the due date. Even where no change is required, the review is documented and the due date updated. Any authorised user

6. Change Control

6.1 Revisions

All content changes require a new revision number and must restart from Step 1 (Draft). Minor editorial corrections that do not alter meaning may be made at Step 3 (Approval) without a full review cycle, at the Authoriser's discretion, with the reason documented.

6.2 Hand Amendment (interim only)

Where a document is amended by hand pending formal re-issue, the change is initialled and dated by the Authoriser; the amended copy is treated as current. A formal revised document following the full workflow must be issued within 30 days.

6.3 Urgent Changes

Where patient safety or a regulatory change requires immediate effect, the Authoriser may issue a documented written instruction. A formally revised document must be issued within 7 days.

7. Obsolescence

When a document is superseded or withdrawn, the Quality Manager moves it to the Archived folder in Nexus 360 and sets its status to Obsolete. Copies retained for legal or knowledge purposes are watermarked OBSOLETE — REFERENCE ONLY and must not be used to guide practice.

8. AASM Annual Policy Review AASM N-2.3

Under AASM accreditation, the Network Director must annually review and provide oversight of all policies and protocols across the accreditation network. This requirement is met by:

  1. Generating a Nexus 360 report of all AASM-mode policies and protocols at the start of each calendar year.
  2. The Network Director reviewing each document for currency, AASM standard alignment, and consistency with active AASM Clinical Practice Guidelines.
  3. Initiating a formal revision (Steps 1–4) for any document found to be out of date or non-compliant.
  4. The Network Director signing and dating a summary attestation confirming the annual review is complete. This record is retained in Nexus 360 for a minimum of five years.

9. Records

The following records are maintained in Nexus 360: master controlled-document register (REG-001); FRM-027 Document Change Requests; periodic review records; issue/notification log; AASM annual review attestations.
