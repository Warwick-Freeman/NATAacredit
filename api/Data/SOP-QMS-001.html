1. Purpose ASA 4.2

To establish, implement and maintain a documented quality management system (QMS) that covers all activities of the service, complies with the ASA Standard for Sleep Disorders Services and ISO 15189:2012, and supports continual improvement of patient care.

2. Scope

Applies to all sites, all study modalities (attended PSG, home/ambulatory, CPAP titration, MSLT/MWT, paediatric) and all permanent and offsite activities, including home-based services and any subcontracted work.

3. Responsibilities

RoleResponsibility
Medical DirectorUltimate accountability for QMS; chairs management review; authorises the Quality Manual.
Quality ManagerMaintains the QMS, document control, audit programme and CAPA register; direct access to top management.
Senior Scientist/TechnologistOwns technical QMS elements (equipment, methods, QC).
All staffComply with the QMS, contribute suggestions, report nonconformances.

4. Quality Policy

The service is committed to: (a) delivering sleep diagnostic and therapy services consistent with current best practice and the ASA Standard; (b) the safety, privacy and welfare of patients; (c) continual improvement of the QMS; (d) competence and ongoing development of staff; and (e) responsiveness to referrers and patients. The Medical Director endorses this policy annually.

5. Quality Objectives

  • >=98% of final reports issued within 10 business days of patient contact.
  • >=95% of urgent (safety-critical OSA) referrals studied within 4 weeks.
  • 100% of equipment with verification within scheduled cycle.
  • >=2 proficiency-testing rounds per analysing staff member per year.
  • Internal audit cycle completed within rolling 12 months.
  • Management review held at <=12-month interval.

6. QMS Structure

  1. Quality Manual - this and the Quality Policy are the apex documents.
  2. Policies & SOPs - controlled per SOP-QMS-002.
  3. Forms & records - managed per SOP-QMS-003.
  4. External standards & references - ASA Standard, AASM Manual, ISO 15189, RACP curricula, ASA Guidelines.

7. Communication of the QMS

All staff are inducted into the QMS at orientation, receive updates at quarterly staff meetings, and acknowledge controlled-document revisions via the document register.

8. Related Documents

9. References
