1. Purpose

Explicit criteria for defining the patient population evaluated to ensure all patient evaluations are within the scope of the professional expertise, technical competence and capability of the staff.

2. Policy

The sleep clinic will evaluate, manage and provide follow-up for an adequate range of sleep disorders as defined in the most current edition of the International Classification of Sleep Disorders meeting certain criteria established in adherence to all applicable current AASM guidelines.

Patients complaining of a potential sleep disorder or sleep disturbance will be scheduled for an evaluation with a clinic medical staff member. While most of the patients seen are adults, pediatric patients beginning at the age of (Insert minimum age of acceptance) are accepted for a physician evaluation.

Patients experiencing the following symptoms will be evaluated by an appropriate medical staff provider:

hypersomnia

excessive daytime sleepiness

insomnia

leg movement problems

snoring

parasomnias

other symptoms that may interfere with daytime activities

Patients who have certain psychiatric or other medical conditions may be excluded based upon medical staff decisions or referred to an appropriate medical provider.

3. Procedure

  1. Clinic staff will respond to all requests for evaluations, consultations, or follow-ups.
    1. The date of birth will be obtained; the age will be reviewed to determine if the minimum age requirement has been met.
    2. Patients beginning at the age of 13 will be accepted.
    3. If the minimum age requirement is not met, the child will be referred to a clinic specializing in age-appropriate management.

Screening information is obtained to ensure that a sleep disorder is within the scope of the clinic. If the sleep disorder can be managed by the clinic, the patient can be processed and scheduled for a clinic visit.

Clinic staff will begin the insurance authorization process prior to scheduling the evaluation.

Self-referrals are accepted once insurance coverage is verified.

The clinic medical staff member will evaluate all patients and offer HSAT to those individuals who meet the HSAT Acceptance Criteria and are deemed clinically appropriate candidates to be further evaluated with HSAT (reference HSAT acceptance policy).

A physician order will be obtained for those patients directly referred (only being referred for testing; not scheduled or expected to be seen by a clinic medical staff member). A comprehensive history and physical relating to sleep complaints must be submitted and approved by the appropriately trained approved staff member prior to sleep testing.

The history and physical must conform to current AASM clinical practice guidelines. If the physician is ordering the study for other reasons, the history and physical must state why sleep testing is indicated.

A complete list of current medications, including any for allergies, will be obtained.

Other medical conditions of the patient will also be obtained.

A clinic medical staff member or appropriately trained technical or administrative staff member will review the information provided and determine if the requested study is indicated. If indications are in question, the referring physician will be contacted to obtain more information, or the patient will be scheduled for a clinic evaluation.

A copy of all notes and communication with the referring provider will be maintained in the medical record.

After reviewing all information, the request is approved and the HSAT study is scheduled.
