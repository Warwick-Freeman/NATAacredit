1. Purpose

2. A Standard Protocol For Performing The Maintenance Of Wakefulness Test (Mwt) Consistent With The Aasm Clinical Practice Ensures Consistency Among Technicians, Comparisons Between Tests And Interpretation Consistent With Published Data.

3. Policy

The sleep facility will perform MWTs in a standardized manner consistent with the AASM Practice for Clinical Use of the Multiple Sleep Latency Test and the Maintenance of Wakefulness Test.

General Considerations

4. The Mwt Measures Alertness And The Ability To Stay Awake. It Is Indicated To Assess A Person’S Ability To Remain Awake When The Inability To Remain Awake Constitutes A Public Or Personal Safety Issue. Mwt May Be Indicated To Determine The Effect Of Medications, Substances, Or Other Interventions Compared To Pretreatment Or To Determine The Patient’S Pharmacological State Compared To Normal Controls.

5. Procedure

  1. Clinical Guidance & Patient Preparation

In preparation, the clinician and the patient should define goals for adequate sleep regarding timing and duration. Adequate sleep should be documented by sleep diary and, whenever possible, actigraphy for 2 weeks before testing.

The MWT should be conducted when a patient is clinically stable and when treatments of any known sleep disorders are well-established and effective.

In patients with sleep-disordered breathing who are being evaluated for the effectiveness of therapy, the clinician should ensure effectiveness (efficacy and adherence) based on a review of downloaded data or self-reported use for non-PAP before testing. If adequate effectiveness is suboptimal, then the clinician should determine if the anticipated impact on the test results warrants rescheduling. The patient should use PAP/non-PAP therapy on the night before the MWT.

The clinician should develop a plan regarding the use of prescription medications, over-the-counter (OTC) agents, herbal remedies, and other substances. If the patient is chronically taking medications with alerting or sedating properties, then they should be continued at a stable dose. Changes in medications should be avoided for 2 weeks before testing. The patient should be instructed to consult with the clinician before starting a prescription or OTC medication before the test.

The ordering clinician in consultation with the patient should clarify acceptable caffeine consumption before the MWT.

  1. General Testing Procedures

Relevant clinical data such as preceding sleep schedules, PAP adherence, or other therapies should be available to the interpreting clinician. The MWT should be performed after the patient’s major sleep period. Performance of a PSG before the MWT is at the discretion of the sleep clinician.

The patient’s clothing should be comfortable, be appropriate to the environment, and not interfere with the performance of tests. If a PSG is performed, then a change in clothing is not required between the PSG and the MWT.

The patient should abstain from alcohol, marijuana, and other sedating substances on the day of the test.

Patients on PAP/non-PAP therapies for sleep-disordered breathing should use them the night before (but not during) the MWT. If a PSG is performed, then the PAP settings and mask interface should match those used at home.

The recording montage for the MWT should, at a minimum, include 3 EEG recording leads with 1 each for frontal (F3-M2 or F4-M1), central (C3-M2 or C4-M1), and occipital (O1-M2 or O2-M1) derivations, left and right eye EOGs, mental/submental EMG, and EKG. Other recording devices or sensors used for the PSG are unnecessary and should be removed to promote patient comfort. The use of alternate acceptable montages in the current version of the AASM Scoring Manual are at the discretion of the sleep clinician.

A 50-microvolt standard calibration is performed on all recording channels.

The electrodes should be inspected for good adherence, and any loose electrodes should be replaced.

An impedance check should be performed, and any electrodes >5000 ohms should be replaced and rechecked.

Audiovisual recordings must be made during the wake trials and be accessible to interpreting sleep specialists. The patient must be audiovisually monitored throughout the day, but retention of recordings made between trials is discretionary.

The MWT should consist of four 40-minute wake trials. The initial trial should begin 1.5 to 3 hours after termination of the preceding night’s sleep at home, and a subsequent trial should begin 2 hours after the start of the prior trial.

Before each wake trial, the patient should be offered the use of the restroom and queried regarding other requirements for comfort.

Sleep rooms should be dimly lit, quiet, and at a comfortable temperature during testing. The light source should deliver an illuminance of 0.1 to 0.13 lux at the corneal level (such as a 7.5-watt nightlight) placed 12 inches off the floor and 3 feet lateral to the patient’s head. The patient should be given sufficient time to acclimate to the recording room before the start of the first trial.

The patient should be seated in a bed or reclining chair with the back and head comfortably supported. This should be the same for all wake trials.

Patient biocalibration should be conducted before starting each wake trial. Standard instructions include:

14.1. “Sit quietly with your eyes open for 30 seconds.”

14.2. “Close both eyes for 30 seconds.”

14.3. “Without moving your head, look to the right, then left, then right, then left, right and then left.”

14.4 “Blink eyes slowly for 5 times.”

14.5 “Clench or grit your teeth tightly together.”

At the start of each wake trial, the patient should be instructed as follows: “Please sit still and remain awake for as long as possible. Look directly ahead of you, and do not look directly at the light.”

Testing starts immediately after instructions are given, and bedroom lights are turned off.

Each wake trial is ended once the patient has 3 consecutive epochs of stage N1 sleep or 1 epoch of any other sleep stage or after 40 minutes.

Stimulating activities such as consuming nicotine and the use of electronic devices and cell phones should end at least 30 minutes before each wake trial. Vigorous physical activity and prolonged exposures to sunlight/bright artificial light should be avoided all day.

Between wake trials, the patient should be out of bed and not permitted to sleep.

A light breakfast at least 1 hour before the first wake trial and a light lunch immediately after the termination of the second wake trial are recommended.

Urine drug screening should be employed when indicated to ensure that the MWT results are not confounded by inadvertent, intentional, or illicit medication or substance use.

  1. Data Acquisition & Reporting
  2. Data acquisition and reporting include the following:

patient demographics (name, DOB, test date, BMI, medical record number)

names of referring clinician, sleep specialist, and sleep technologist

documentation of medications used within 24 hours of and during the MWT and any changes to medications within last 2 weeks. If performed, the type of drug screening should be documented

documentation of available prestudy data including sleep diary, actigraphy, and PAP download

recording parameters including start time, end time, total sleep time, and sleep latency of each wake trial Sleep latency is defined as the time from lights-out until the start of the first epoch of any stage of sleep (an epoch of N1, N2, N3, or R)

mean sleep latency averaged over the 4 wake trials If no sleep occurs on a trial, then 40 minutes is used as the sleep latency and in the calculation of the mean sleep latency

eviations from ideal testing times and conditions (e.g., nicotine, caffeine, napping, cell phone, fire alarms, or other stimulating activities), should be documented by the sleep technologist

nterpretation of study findings with signature of board-certified sleep medicine physician

  1. End of Study

At the end of the last trial, turn off polygraph.

Gently remove all sensors from the patient, take care to avoid irritation of patient’s skin.

Carefully remove tape and electrodes from the patient’s skin.

Ensure all paste residue has been removed by using a damp wash cloth on the skin and a fine-toothed comb through the hair after all electrodes have been removed.

When the patient is ready to leave, the technologist should recommend that the patient schedule a follow-up visit with his or physician to discuss the results of the study. Then discharge the patient from the facility.

  1. After the Study

Carefully sort wires and group them together by lengths and application sites.

Remove any remaining tape, wash electrodes with soap and water, rinse and allow to soak in Wex-Cide solution for a minimum of 10 minutes. Rinse well and allow it to dry.

Inspect wires to ensure their integrity.

Return any equipment and all cleaned and disinfected wires to the storage area for future use.

General Cleanup Checklist

Discard all used tape, collars, gauze, etc.

Return patient preparation kit to appropriate area.

Stock patient preparation kit as needed.

If CPAP and/or oxygen equipment was used, remove and empty humidifier, connecting tubing, nasal cannula, and any other equipment and place in designated “dirty equipment area” for cleaning and disinfecting.

Discard disposable equipment such as the nasal cannula or disposable oximeter probe.

Remove any lint from CPAP equipment filter.

Remove used linen and place in appropriate dirty linen container.

Leave patient suites in clean and orderly condition.

Scoring the MWT

Sleep Stage Scoring is based upon the most current version of the AASM Scoring Manual.

Sleep latency is determined from lights-out to the first scored epoch of any stage of sleep.
