1. Purpose ASA 4.13

To define how the service identifies, collects, indexes, accesses, stores, maintains and disposes of records, and to set retention periods consistent with legislation and contractual obligations.

2. Scope

Applies to all record types: quality records (audits, CAPA, complaints, training, management reviews, suggestions, supplier evaluations), technical records (equipment, calibration, QC, proficiency testing), and patient records (referrals, raw data, video/audio, reports, correspondence).

3. Identification

Each record set carries: a minimum of three patient identifiers (where patient-related), unique study/service ID, date, type of record/study, identity of the person making each entry (cl. 4.13.1.3), and a link to the referral.

4. Retention Schedule

5. Storage & Access

All records are held securely and confidentially. Electronic records reside in the encrypted QMS repository with role-based access; paper records are stored in locked cabinets in access-controlled rooms. Access logs are retained.

6. Corrections

Records are indelible. When mistakes occur, the entry is crossed out (not erased), the correct value entered alongside, and the change initialled and dated by the person making it (cl. 4.13.2.7). Electronic systems retain prior values via audit trail.

7. Disposal

At end of retention, paper records are shredded by a contracted secure-destruction provider; electronic records are securely deleted including from backups. A disposal log is maintained.

8. Legal Hold

Records subject to litigation, investigation or subpoena are flagged "Legal Hold" and exempted from disposal until released by the Medical Director.

9. References

ASA Standard cl. 4.13.1 - 4.13.2.7; relevant state Health Records Acts.
