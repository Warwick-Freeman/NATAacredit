1. Purpose ASA 5.6

To monitor the validity of sleep studies and services through internal QC, performance indicators and external proficiency testing.

2. Quality Indicators

The service maintains a catalogue of indicators covering pre-study, study and post-study processes (FRM-019). Indicators have stated objective, methodology, target, threshold, action plan and review period. Examples include:

  • % reports within 10 business days (cl. 5.8.1).
  • % urgent referrals studied within 4 weeks.
  • Inter-observer scoring concordance (kappa).
  • Equipment verification on-time %.
  • CPAP adherence at 30/90/365 days.
  • Complaint rate per 1,000 studies.

3. Internal Quality Control

  1. 10% of completed studies reviewed by Tech Lead for technical quality.
  2. Inter-observer variability assessed quarterly: blinded re-score of N=5 studies per scorer; report kappa.
  3. Administrative review of every issued report for completeness.
  4. QC results recorded; remedial action raised as NC where required.

4. External Proficiency Testing

  1. Every analysing staff member participates in an external PT programme at least twice per year.
  2. Results imported to FRM-020; out-of-spec results raise CAPA.
  3. Where no formal PT exists, alternative arrangements used: exchange of studies with another service; re-analysis of previously analysed studies. Evidence retained.
  4. Physicians participate in documented peer concordance review.

5. Records

Indicator dashboard; QC review log; PT results; concordance records; CAPA links.

6. References

ASA Standard cl. 5.6.1 - 5.6.8.4.
