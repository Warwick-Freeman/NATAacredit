1. Purpose ASA 4.8

To ensure complaints from clinicians, patients, staff or third parties are received, investigated, responded to and used to improve the service.

ℹ️ Nexus 360 Integration: Steps in this procedure that are performed or supported by the Nexus 360 quality management system are identified with [Nexus 360] throughout this document. Staff must access Nexus 360 at http://localhost:5173 to complete these steps. The system audit trail satisfies record-keeping requirements for all digitally recorded activities.

2. Channels

Verbal, written, email, web form, survey. Every staff member is empowered to receive a complaint.

3. Procedure

  1. Complaint logged in Nexus 360 NC & CAPA module [Nexus 360] within 1 working day of receipt (FRM-011 supplements as required); acknowledgement to complainant within 3 working days.
  2. Quality Manager triages severity (Minor / Significant / Major / Sentinel) and assigns Investigator.
  3. Investigation completed; root cause analysis where Significant or above; clinical safety review where patient harm possible.
  4. Response letter to complainant signed by Medical Director where Significant or above; otherwise by Quality Manager. Target response time: 10 working days (Minor), 20 working days (Significant), 30 working days (Major) - with interim updates.
  5. Corrective action raised in NC/CAPA register where systemic cause identified.
  6. Closure recorded on FRM-011; complainant offered escalation pathway (eg HCCC).

4. Confidentiality & Reprisal

Complainant identity protected to the extent possible. No reprisal against staff who raise complaints in good faith.

5. Trend Review

Monthly complaints summary by category and root-cause theme; tabled at management review.

6. Records

NC/CAPA records in Nexus 360 [Nexus 360]; FRM-011 (supplementary); all correspondence; trend reports from Nexus 360 Quality Indicators module [Nexus 360].

7. References

ASA Standard cl. 4.8.1.
