1. Purpose

To provide explicit criteria for defining the patient population evaluated to ensure all patient evaluations are within the scope of the professional expertise, technical competence, and capability of the staff.

2. Policy

It is the policy to provide HSAT to patients who meet the acceptance criteria in adherence to the AASM Clinical Practice Parameters.

3. Procedure

Follow patient acceptance of sleep clinic and in-lab.

A sleep medicine evaluation consistent with AASM practice standards, including indications for testing, physical exam, a complete list of current medications, allergies, and a complete list of other medical conditions is required upon scheduling of the HSAT device.

HSAT acceptance criteria

Patients 18 years and older will be tested using HSAT.

Patients tested with HSAT must have a high pretest probability of OSA with limited comorbidities.

The following may exclude a patient from acceptance for HSAT:

moderate to severe pulmonary disease

chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD)

emphysema

asthma

Neuromuscular conditions

multiple sclerosis

parkinson’s disease

muscular dystrophy

Congestive heart failure

Other sleep disorders

central sleep apnea

periodic leg movement disorder (PLMD)

circadian rhythm disorder (CRD)

narcolepsy

parasomnias

HSAT is not used for general screening of asymptomatic populations.

HSAT may be indicated for patients for whom in-center testing is not possible due to immobility, safety, or critical illness.

HSAT may be indicated to monitor the response to non-PAP treatments for OSA, including:

oral appliances

upper airway surgery

weight loss

If health insurance requires HSAT that does not adhere to the AASM clinical guidelines, notification will be documented in the medical record.
