1. Purpose

Polysomnographic studies are performed on patients to diagnose a variety of sleep disorders when ordered by a sleep staff physician or by another physician, with the approval of the director or an appropriately licensed medical staff member. The following protocol for polysomnography is consistent with the current version of the AASM Practice Parameters for the Indications for Polysomnography and Related Procedures.

2. Policy

All recording devices in application of such devices will be conducted according to the specifications detailed in the most current version of the AASM Scoring Manual for Sleep and Associated Events.

Possible indications for polysomnography include:

sleep-related breathing disorders

positive airway pressure titration (CPAP, BiPAP, ASV) in patients with sleep-related breathing disorders

titration of implanted neurostimulation devices for treatment of obstructive and central sleep apnea

prior to a multiple sleep latency test in the evaluation of suspected narcolepsy

sleep-related behaviors that are violent or otherwise potentially injurious to the patient or others.

unusual parasomnias

neuromuscular disorders with sleep-related symptoms

paroxysmal arousals or other sleep disruptions thought to be seizure related

presumed parasomnia or sleep related seizure disorder that does not respond to conventional therapy

significant periodic limb movement disorder

3. Procedure

Recorded parameters:

electroencephalogram (EEG) (frontal, central, occipital)

electrooculogram EOG (left, right)

chin electromyography (EMG)

leg EMG (left, right)

airflow

respiratory effort

oxygen saturation

body position

ECG

Upon admission to the sleep facility, each patient will have an assessment completed by the sleep lab technologist for data collection and to determine any immediate needs or concerns.

Admission assessment will include the following:

review of demographic information

reason for sleep study

physiological parameters (e.g., height)

current medications

environment—special needs of the patient (e.g., hearing aid, glasses, cane, interpreter)

fall risk assessment

patient/family education

discharge planning: Where? With whom?

reminder to patient of follow-up appointment with referring physician

Step-by-step directions:

Have all equipment cleaned and ready for use before the patient arrives.

Inspect all electrodes and recording devices for integrity.

Instruct the patient to change into their bedtime clothes.

Instruct the patient to sit in the chair for hookup.

Explain the function of each recording device as it is applied.

Clean the site of each electrode on the patient before placement.

Fill each electrode cup with conductive paste.

Attach the EEG sensors and the ground (F4, C4, O2, and M1). Backup electrodes should be placed at F3, C3, O1, and M2

Place the left electrooculogram electrode (EOG) 1 cm below and 1 cm lateral to the left outer canthus.

Place the right electrooculogram electrode (EOG) 1 cm above and 1 cm lateral to the right outer canthus.

Place the right electrooculogram electrode (EOG). Place three electrodes to record chin EMG: one in the midline 1 cm above the inferior edge of the mandible, one 2 cm below the inferior edge of the mandible and 2 cm to the right of the midline, one 2 cm below the inferior edge of the mandible and 2 cm to the left of the midline.

Place single modified electrocardiograph leads on torso.

Place ventilatory effort bands (RIP belts or PVDF belts) above the breastbone and around the midline of the abdomen. Ensure there is a separation between the bands.

Attach the pulse oximeter.

Prep the patient’s legs with alcohol for the EMG electrodes placement. Place the EMG electrodes on the left and right anterior tibialis muscles.

Place the airflow sensor (thermistor, nasal pressure, end-tidal carbon dioxide (ETCO2) directly in the path of the patient’s airflow. Secure sensor with tape.

Tape the snore microphone on the front of the neck left or right of the area of thyroid cartilage. (Adam’s apple, in males).

Connect all patient cables headbox and oximetry probe.

Instruct patient to lay supine and very still, with eyes open.

Perform biocalibrations prior to lights-out at night and after lights on in the morning.

Review recordings for artifacts, adjust electrodes/sensors or digital settings as needed.

If all-night CPAP titration is ordered, fit patient with an appropriate mask and begin CPAP therapy. See CPAP Titration policy and procedure.

Instruct patient that they may watch TV or read until lights-out at night.

If the patient wants to go to sleep, the lights are turned off.

Begin testing.

Enter tags into the computer; lights-out, body position, etc., every 30 minutes or in response to all atypical events.

Patients will be awakened at the end of study.

Patients will be given an end-of-study questionnaire.

Patients will be allowed to shower if desired.

Patients who are to undergo a multiple sleep latency test (MSLT) will be handed off to the day technician.

Room and equipment will be processed for the next patient per the infection control policy.

Final interpretation will be completed by an appropriately licensed physician and will be reviewed by an individual board-certified in sleep medicine.
