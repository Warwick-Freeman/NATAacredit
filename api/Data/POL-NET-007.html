1. Purpose

To improve patient care, process, and clinical outcomes, the network and all its services will be committed to continuous quality improvement.

2. Policy

Quality improvement measures will be assessed quarterly and reported by the network director. The technical director will contribute to the development and reporting of activities as appropriate and will support the department as needed. Additional individuals may be involved in data gathering and reporting based on the organizational structure.

The quality assurance (QA) program will select two outcome and one process measures to gauge the effectiveness of patient care improvements. At least three sleep medicine measures will be monitored.

The network director, alongside the technical director, will establish and execute the QA program. Quarterly, the network director will validate the efficacy of all quality improvement endeavors and address any measures that fall below established thresholds.

Annually, the network director will evaluate the QA program's effectiveness to identify additional measures as necessary for performance review.

3. Procedure

  1. The QA plan will measure clinical processes and patient outcomes. Measures will be identified and chosen, through clinical and administrative collaboration. Examples of these measures are available at https://aasm.org/clinical-resources/practice-standards/quality-measures/.
  2. Collection and Review of Data
    1. Monitoring and collection of data will be performed monthly to identify variances from the established criteria.
    2. Data will be collected using a medical record audit tool.
    3. Results will be measured and analyzed.
    4. Measures that have failed, met, or exceeded their established threshold will be identified.
    5. All measures will be further discussed and evaluated.
    6. Evaluation and Reporting
    7. Results of the measures should be tracked quarterly, aggregated, and reported to the director.
    8. The network director will review and report all measures to determine if the minimum expected thresholds are met.
    9. Evaluation shall focus on identifying opportunities to improve the process and outcomes of patient care by addressing identified problems that result in negative outcomes.
    10. The director will evaluate, summarize and draw conclusions on the presented data, providing recommendations for remediation if measured thresholds are not met.
    11. Written summary reports of all measures must be signed and dated by the network director.
    12. Results of measures relevant to the technical staff will be reported at their monthly staff meeting (documented in Nexus 360).
    13. 3.7. Written summary reports will be retained in Nexus 360 for a minimum of 5 years.
      1. Action
    14. Upon review of the data, conclusions, and recommendations, actions will be directed to resolve identified problems by using resources, committees, and problem-solving techniques:
      1. The network director will review and determine the areas requiring improvement.
      2. A remediation plan will be created.
      3. Actions will be taken as appropriate to implement the resolution.
      4. Other measures may be chosen as warranted.
