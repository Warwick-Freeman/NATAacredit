1. Purpose

  1. To ensure staff, patients/caregivers are provided with the appropriate education for the proper setup, use and maintenance of positive airway pressure (PAP) equipment.

2. Policy

It is the policy to train all durable medical equipment (DME) staff to ensure the safe and efficient delivery of medical equipment, emphasizing patient safety. Ongoing continuing education is required annually.

All patients/caregivers, either upon initial setup or on an ongoing basis, are given instruction/education by a respiratory therapist (RRT, CRT) in the safe use and operations of their equipment. Education may be in the form of verbal instruction, instructional handouts, or written material.

3. Procedure

The RRT or (insert other delivery personnel title) providing instruction on the equipment will:

Be instructed on all aspects of the specific brand of equipment

type of equipment

delivery procedures, set up procedures

possess and demonstrates the knowledge and ability to properly teach and evaluate patients on PAP therapy

have participated in at least 30 credits (averaged 10 per year over the past 36 months) of continuing education (CE) activities on sleep, respiratory therapy, or other related topics

All credits obtained will be recorded in the employee file.

possess and maintain appropriate licensure where required by state law

Patient/caregiver(s) will be given education:

during new or initial setup

patient or family request

upon physician or other health care professional request

if observed difficulty upon reassessment of patient

Patients will be instructed in the safe and effective operations of the specific equipment to include:

physician’s order requirements applicable to the PAP equipment

the purpose function and operations of PAP equipment

instructions on how to use the equipment

necessity for proper mask/pillow fitting

instructions on how to clean the equipment

requirements for regular maintenance and schedule for replacements

instructions on how to contact supplier for routine supply, replacement needs

what to do if equipment does not function properly

contact accessibility and availability for equipment needs in emergency situations

Patients will be given both verbal and written information regarding 24 hours, 7 days a week, 52 weeks per year availability and accessibility.
