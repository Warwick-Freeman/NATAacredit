1. Purpose

To ensure the availability and readiness of appropriate medical emergency equipment in the event of required use. To provide staff with the training necessary to respond to a cardiopulmonary medical emergency.

2. Policy

It is the policy that appropriate emergency medical equipment will be available and accessible to all staff in the event of a cardiopulmonary event and maintained in accordance with the manufacturer’s recommendations. All personnel will take part in an annual cardiopulmonary drill using the AED. If all personnel cannot attend the drill, more than one drill may need to be arranged. Attendance will be recorded, and a copy will be maintained in the individuals the Nexus 360 Staff & Training module.

3. Procedure

Equipment used in an emergency (AED, Barrier for resuscitation, oxygen) is located in the __Nexus 360 ______.

All staff will be required to be trained annually in the use of emergency equipment (AED). This will include conducting an annual cardiopulmonary drill.

Plan the emergency drill:

Schedule a date and time for the practice drill.

Key individuals responsible for the coordination/facilitation of the drill must be assigned.

Facilitator identifies all the players in the drill.

Assign sleep technicians to sleep rooms for purposes of the drill:

Tech #1 is assigned to testing room 1 and 2.

Tech #2 assigned to testing room 3 and 4 (continued as applicable).

One individual observer places manikin at the designated time in the designated sleep room.

One individual is responsible for starting the clock and recording time points and responses (complete the checklist).

Determine the scenario that will be used for the drill.

Option 1—During a scheduled PSG an adult becomes unresponsive, vital signs absent, patient is in cardiac arrest.

Option 2—During split night study patient found in ventricular fibrillation.

Notify the team that a drill may take place within a couple of weeks to allow them time to review the emergency protocols.

Do not disclose the exact date or time.

Instruct the staff to respond to the drill as if it was a real emergency.

Ensure equipment/supplies are available, including:

clothed manikin to represent the patient

AED

stethoscope

EKG monitor

CPR masks

oxygen

stopwatch

Perform the drill.

A manikin is placed in the designated sleep testing room.

Facilitator calls out “patient unresponsive in cardiac arrest; call 911.”

Observer starts the time clock and fills out the checklist recording all the times when each event occurs.

After the drill

Discuss the drill with the sleep team to gain feedback from staff and discuss/address any concerns.

Inform the team of the evaluation process.

Evaluate the Drill

What did the sleep team do right?

What could have been done better?

Provide a Summary of the Evaluation

areas that went well

areas for improvement/recommendation

Schedule remediation if necessary.

Decide if repeat drill will be necessary.

Determine if all other applicable emergency procedures were followed.

contact procedures

access to front door for ER personnel

The other patient was monitored.

Maintenance of Emergency Equipment

Staff will check to ensure the AED light is flashing “ready” daily and document on the AED check log the “light is on.”

An authorized clinic staff member (insert name) _________ will inspect the expiration dates of the AED battery and pads, monthly and ensure the supply bag attached contains all necessary supplies (CPR mask, razor, dry cloth and scissors).

Any reordering of supplies is to be done in advance.
