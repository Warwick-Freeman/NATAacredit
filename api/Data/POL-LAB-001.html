1. Purpose

To establish explicit criteria for defining the patient population evaluated, ensuring that all patient evaluations fall within the scope of professional expertise, technical competence, and capability of the staff.

2. Policy

It is the policy to accept, evaluate, and treat all patients with sleep-related disorders meeting certain established criteria.

In-Lab Acceptance Criteria

Patients beginning at the age of __(insert minimum age)_years of age will be tested.

Patients may be self-referred or physician referred.

Patients with sleep complaints (e.g. sleep-related breathing, insomnia, daytime sleepiness etc.).

Patients requiring the services of a caregiver during the night will be required to bring someone with them who can stay throughout the procedures to provide the needed assistance.

The following may exclude a patient from acceptance.

Potential patients with untreated psychiatric or medical conditions that would affect the validity of the study or endanger the technical staff, based on review of the case by the director or designated sleep staff physician.

Patients whose weight exceeds 400 pounds.

3. Procedure

Sleep staff will respond to all requests for outpatient consultations or sleep studies by filling out a new patient form.

Patients will be required to complete preadmission documentation, which may include medical history forms, sleep diaries, and other relevant information.

The date of birth will be obtained; the age will be reviewed to determine if the minimum age requirement has been met.

If the minimum age requirement is not met, the child will be referred to a sleep facility that conducts testing on pediatric patients.

For children under the age of 18, the caller will be informed that a parent or guardian must stay with the individual through the testing procedure.

For those patients directly referred (only being referred for testing), not scheduled or expected to be seen by a clinic medical staff member before or after the study, a physician’s order will be obtained. A comprehensive history and physical relating to sleep complaints must be submitted and approved by the appropriately trained approved staff member prior to sleep testing.

The history and physical must conform to the AASM clinical practice guidelines. If the physician is ordering the study for other reasons, the history and physical must state why the sleep test is indicated.

A complete list of current medications, including any for allergies, will be obtained.

Other medical conditions of the patient will also be obtained.

The director or an appropriately licensed designated staff member will determine if the patient has any psychiatric or medical condition that prevents the patient from being accepted for a sleep study presently.
