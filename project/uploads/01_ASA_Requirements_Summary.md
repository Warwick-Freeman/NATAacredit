# ASA Standard for Sleep Disorders Services (March 2019) - Requirements Summary

**Source:** ASA Standard for Sleep Disorders Services, December 2018 / March 2019 final, 36 pages.
**Purpose of this document:** Plain-English digest of every clause that the proposed accreditation web app must support, organised so each requirement maps to one or more system features.

The Standard derives from **ISO 15189:2012** and the **ASA Adult & Paediatric Sleep Curricula / Guidelines** and is the criterion document used by NATA assessors under the **ASA/NATA Sleep Disorders Services Accreditation Program**.

---

## 1. Scope of services to support
The system must accommodate any combination of:
- Attended (in-laboratory) Polysomnography (PSG, Type 1) - adult and paediatric.
- Unattended / home / ambulatory studies (Type 2, 3, 4 devices).
- CPAP / PAP titration and efficacy studies (including bi-level, split-night, auto-titration alternatives).
- MSLT and MWT.
- Ongoing PAP / oral appliance / surgical / behavioural therapy follow-up.
- Paediatric sleep diagnostics and non-invasive / invasive ventilation management.

---

## 2. Section 4 - Management requirements (mandatory clauses)

### 4.1 Organisation & management responsibility
- Service is a legal entity; organisational chart endorsed and current.
- Resources adequate for case-mix and workload.
- Regular minuted scheduled meetings (planning, QA, clinical review, education, research where applicable).
- Defined roles: Medical Director (sleep physician), Paediatric Sleep Physician (where children seen), Senior Scientist/Technologist, Quality Manager, deputies.
- Documented duties, lines of accountability, conflict-of-interest declarations, confidentiality controls.
- Rostering rules - eg adult: ratio not worse than 1 tech : 3 patients overnight, >=45 min prep, >=2 h analysis per study; paediatric: 1 tech : 2 patients, 1-2 h prep, 4 h analysis; Medical Director on-site >=12 h/month; Senior Scientist/Technologist on-site >=20 h/week (full-time service).
- Staff awareness records of QMS objectives.

### 4.2 Quality management system (QMS)
- Documented QMS covering all sites including home services.
- Quality manual referencing quality policy, scope, structure, roles, document hierarchy.
- Measurable quality objectives; staff trained in manual use.

### 4.3 Document control
- Master list of controlled documents; approval, periodic review, removal of obsolete copies, marked archived copies.
- Unique IDs (title, edition/date/revision, page x of y, issuing authority).
- Defined procedures for hand amendments and electronic-document changes.

### 4.4 Review of appropriateness of referral & patient preparation
- Sources/types of referrals appropriate; clinical evaluation prior to study.
- Sleep physician triage; paediatric patients evaluated by paediatric sleep physician.
- Document deviations from referral; subcontract or onward-refer where further assessment needed.

### 4.5 Subcontracting / onward referral
- Register of subcontractors; written notice to referring doctor; service responsible for subcontracted work.
- Third-party scoring/reporting requires QA program (proficiency testing, non-conformance handling, peer concordance).

### 4.6 External services & supplies
- Documented selection / evaluation procedures for purchased services, equipment, consumables.

### 4.7 Feedback
- Two-way referrer/patient feedback (eg surveys), positive and negative.

### 4.8 Complaints
- Documented complaints procedure; record of complaints, investigations, corrective actions.

### 4.9 Identification & control of nonconformities
- Procedure covering responsibility, action, clinical significance, halting/withholding, immediate action, recall, authorisation to resume, trend review.

### 4.10 Corrective action
- Root-cause analysis, plan, implementation, recording, effectiveness review.

### 4.11 Preventive action
- Proactive review of data for potential problems; documented procedure with effectiveness review.

### 4.12 Continual improvement
- Driven by quality policy, objectives, audits, data analysis, CAPA, management review.

### 4.13 Quality and technical records
- Documented procedure for identification, indexing, storage, disposal.
- Retention: equipment records = life + 7 years (or longer per legislation).
- Records identify the person making each entry.
- Each record set must include: referral with clinical details, >=3 patient identifiers, unique study ID, date, study type, original observations and calculations, identity of those performing components, raw data retained at minimum until reporting and treatment complete (preferably for full health-record period); video/audio retained at minimum until final report (longer if clinically diagnostic).
- Indelible records with audit-trail style corrections (cross-out + signature + date).

### 4.14 Evaluation & audits
- Internal audit programme (cycle ~12 months) covering pre-study, study, post-study processes.
- Authorised periodic review of study mix vs referrals.
- Suggestion scheme; risk-based modification of processes; trained, impartial auditors.

### 4.15 Management review (>=annual)
- Inputs: referral review, feedback, suggestions, audits, risk management, quality indicators, external assessments, EQA results, complaints, supplier performance, NCs, CAPA status, prior review follow-ups, scope/staff/premises changes, improvement recommendations.
- Outputs: documented decisions on QMS effectiveness, service improvements, resourcing.
- Findings reported to staff; defined timeframes for actions.

## 3. Section 5 - Technical requirements

### 5.1 Staff
- Qualifications appropriate to task; supervision while training.
- Annual basic life support competence (paediatric BLS for paediatric labs).
- Staff appraisal system with written reports and development plans.
- Orientation, continuing education, attendance at professional meetings.
- Documented job descriptions; senior medical staff per RACP/ASA criteria.
- Senior tech staff: >=2 years experience, tertiary biological/physical degree; RPSGT preferred for larger services.
- Training records (with overseas qualification recognition); competence assessed initially and periodically.

### 5.2 Accommodation & environment
- Facilities meet OH&S, infection-control, fire/electrical safety; body-protected (Class B) electrical supply; equipment listed on ARTG.
- Attended labs: signage, reception, waiting area, sound-proofed bedrooms (>=2.5 x 3.5 m), adequate lighting, emergency oxygen/suction, resuscitation kit, patient call-system, ability to monitor all live signals from monitoring room, ability to remotely adjust PAP settings, secure storage, on-call medical roster.
- Paediatric labs: child-safe age-appropriate bedding, parent sleep space, paediatric BLS staff on-site, AED, age-appropriate resuscitation equipment, documented paediatric emergency protocols.
- Home-based services: reception/waiting room, private set-up rooms, secure storage, on-call medical roster, BLS-trained staff, documented patient-treatment protocol.

### 5.3 Equipment
- Documented selection/purchase/management procedure; ARTG-listed PSG hardware/software with full raw-signal disclosure.
- Audio-video recording mandatory paediatric, desirable adult.
- Each item uniquely labelled.
- Acceptance testing on installation (including loaned equipment).
- Documented use/safety/maintenance instructions accessible at point of use.
- Verification programme: per-interval physical/biological signal checks; safeguards against tampering; date, result, trend monitoring.
- Pre-study biological signal verification recorded in patient file (or alternative for paediatric where not feasible).
- Preventive maintenance programme; defective equipment removed/labelled; effect on prior studies assessed; decontamination before service.
- Adverse-incident reporting to manufacturer / authorities.
- Equipment register containing: identity, manufacturer/serial, supplier contact, in-service date, location, condition received, manuals, acceptance records, maintenance schedule and history, performance records, faults/repairs - retained life + 7 years.
- Consumables inventory procedure.

### 5.4 Pre-study procedures (referral handling)
- Prompt referral handling; capture of clinical/demographic info if missing.
- Demand-management & prioritisation, including escalation rules (eg suspected significant OSA in safety-critical workers within 4 weeks; high-risk comorbidity).
- Suitability check on presentation; route to medical input if test needs to be varied.
- Patient identification with >=3 identifiers; unique service number or 3-identifier set used throughout.

### 5.5 Sleep disorders services processes
- Documented escalation/emergency protocols.
- Methods consistent with recognised standards (AASM Manual v2.5, ASA guidelines, paediatric ASA/ASTA addendum).
- Studies usually >=8 hours; nap studies generally not for diagnosis (paediatric <12 months exception).
- Attended PSG: full raw-signal disclosure, scoring per AASM/ASA, manual review by physician.
- MSLT/MWT: must be performable in-house or via affiliation; full overnight PSG required before MSLT.
- PAP titration: evidence-based methods, training diary for technologists, higher staffing for bi-level / split-night, documented split-night protocol, alternate methods (eg auto-titration) require performance indicators (eg adherence).
- Unattended studies: device-type triage by physician, full raw-signal disclosure, route to attended study where home study fails; >=1.5 h analysis per Type 2 study.
- Method selection documented (purpose, equipment specs, verification, procedure, troubleshooting, QA, cleaning, infection control, records & reports, normal values).
- New / non-standard methods validated; software changes re-validated against archived study collection; computed derivations validated against manual methods.
- Inter-/intra-observer concordance treated as uncertainty-of-measurement indicator.
- Data control: secure, password-controlled industry-standard storage; backups during active management; report copies retained in secure non-editable form.

### 5.6 Quality assurance / proficiency
- Documented internal QC and external proficiency testing programme.
- Records of QC results, corrective action, trend analysis.
- Administrative review of completed reports.
- Quality indicators across pre/study/post phases; periodic review of indicators.
- Diagnostic services: inter-observer scoring variability assessments.
- Treatment services: indicators eg time-from-diagnosis-to-CPAP, adherence, ESS pre/post, patient outcomes, complaints, outsourced provider feedback.
- External proficiency testing twice/year per analysing staff member; alternative arrangements (eg study exchange) where formal EQA unavailable; physicians participate in peer-reviewed concordance.
- Performance reviewed and discussed; corrective action recorded and effectiveness monitored.

### 5.7 Post-study procedures and ongoing care
- Results explained to patients/families by sleep specialist (face-to-face option available).
- Documented protocols for therapy implementation/follow-up (PAP, oral appliances, surgery, positional, weight, behavioural, lifestyle).
- PAP services: prescription, supply, monitoring, early follow-up, adherence reporting; provisional CPAP prescription policy.
- Paediatric NIV/IV ventilation protocols.
- Documented referral pathways to ENT, dental, faciomaxillary, cardiology, endocrinology, psychiatry, clinical psychology, ICU, etc.
- Insomnia (and paediatric behavioural sleep) management or referral pathway.
- Diagnostic-only services: must advise referrer on suitability, provide comprehensive report, ensure information sufficient to form opinion, provide management advice on request, urgent clinical review (in-house or subcontracted) when severe SDB or acute findings present, raw data available on request.

### 5.8 Reporting of results
- Patient record contains study reports, consultation notes, correspondence, working/final diagnoses, treatment/follow-up recommendations.
- Correspondence (letters, PSG reports) completed within 10 business days of contact.
- Reporting physician reviews raw data epoch-by-epoch with technologist observations available; feedback loop to scoring tech.
- Published normal reference values with uncertainty estimates.
- Final analysis must be manual (computer-aided not sole source); paediatric reports by paediatric sleep physician.
- Documented reporting policy: preliminary/final report authorisation, status flagging, cross-reference between preliminary and final.
- Mandatory report contents: service identifier, site, patient identifiers, study date, report date, technical/clinical observations, results plus interpretive summary signed by sleep physician, conformance with ASA/AASM templates.
- Page x of y on every page; external study results clearly attributed.
- Revised reports: marked as amendments, dated, signed, original retained.
- Secure transmission protocols (verbal release SOP, electronic transmission SOP); reports retained / accessible to accredited service; downloadable only in protected format if web-delivered.

## 4. Cross-cutting digital capabilities required
- Multi-site / multi-modality (in-lab, home, paediatric, treatment) configuration.
- Role-based access control (Medical Director, Paediatric Director, Senior Tech, Quality Manager, Reporting Physician, Scoring Technologist, Reception, Auditor, External Provider).
- Strong audit trail (per 4.13.2.6/2.7 - no destructive edits; reason-for-change capture).
- Privacy & security (Australian Privacy Act, ARTG-listed devices, healthcare retention rules).
- Electronic-signature workflow for preliminary, final, and amended reports (with version retention).
- Reminders & SLA timers for 10-business-day reporting, 4-week safety-critical OSA, equipment verification cycles, audit cycles (~12 months), management review (<=12 months), training expiry, BLS recertification.
- Reporting/export to NATA assessor format (clause-by-clause evidence pack).
