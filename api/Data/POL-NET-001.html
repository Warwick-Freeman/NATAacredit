1. Purpose

To ensure the delivery of high-quality patient care, it is essential for all professional and technical staff to stay informed about developments and advancements in the field of sleep medicine.

2. Policy

Professional and technical staff must participate in an average of 10 hours per year of continuing medical education/continuing education credit (CME/CEC) sleep educational activities. All continuing education activities must be documented, and copies of original certificates maintained in Nexus 360. In the event of a new hire, CME/CEC may be prorated for advanced practice providers and sleep technologists. If staff fall short on continuing education credits, management will aid the individual in crafting a plan to attain the necessary credits.

3. Procedure

  1. Continuing Education
    1. Each staff member will provide copies of original certificates of CME/CECs earned in sleep by
    2. Non-AASM CME/CECs must be earned in sleep medicine and must meet criteria established by the site director (educational sessions) or by a recognized credentialing provider.
    3. Education sessions will be offered monthly during routine staff meetings.
      1. Educational topics must be sleep-related (e.g., CPR and HIPAA are not considered sleep-related CE activities).
      2. Each educational session will be documented separately, detailing the topic, speaker, and objectives.
      3. An attendance log will be maintained, requiring signatures of all attendees. The log will be reviewed, dated, and signed by the director.
      4. A copy of the in-service attendance log will be filed.
      5. Each in-service hour for technical staff is equivalent to 1 CEC unit.
  2. New or newly hired staff members or those not meeting minimum requirements
    1. Newly hired staff members must provide copies of original CECs earned within the previous three years, which will be filed in the the Nexus 360 Staff & Training module.
    2. If CME/CECs have not been acquired, CME/CEC will be prorated based upon the date of hire for advanced practice providers (NP, PA, APRN), medical staff members, and technical staff.
    3. Newly hired advanced practice provider and technical staff will earn 10 CECs per year based on hire anniversary date.
    4. Three months prior to the annual employment review, each staff the Nexus 360 Staff & Training module will be audited to determine the number of CECs earned to date.
    5. For staff not meeting the minimum requirements, a plan will be developed to assist in completing the minimum requirements.
    6. Monthly follow-up communication will occur until all requirements are met.
    7. Noncompliant individuals will face disciplinary action, which may include suspension or termination.
Professional Staff: Network/site director and medical staff members will obtain 30 credits of AMA PRA Category 1 Credits™ (averaged 10 credits per year over the past 36 months) in sleep medicine.
Technical Staff: All sleep technicians and technologists must earn 30 continuing education credits (averaged 10 credits per year over the past 36 months). CE credits must be in sleep-related educational activities.
