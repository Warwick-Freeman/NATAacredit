1. Purpose ASA 4.5

To ensure that subcontracted tests, analyses or services are performed competently and that onward referrals are made appropriately.

2. Definitions

  • Subcontracting - the service engages another party to perform part of its work; the service retains responsibility for the quality of that work.
  • Onward referral - the service refers the patient to another clinician/service to take over an aspect of care; not subcontracting.

3. Procedure - Subcontracting

  1. Subcontractor evaluated using FRM-018 (qualifications, accreditation status, evidence of compliance, references, financial stability).
  2. Approved subcontractor added to the subcontractor register with scope, expiry and renewal trigger.
  3. Referring doctor advised in writing of the subcontracting arrangement and (where appropriate) approval obtained.
  4. Service remains responsible; subcontractor work integrated into the service report with attribution.
  5. Where the subcontractor performs analysis or reporting, the service operates a QA program covering proficiency testing, NC handling and concordance review.
  6. Subcontractor performance reviewed annually and at management review.

4. Procedure - Onward Referral

Where a service offers limited specialty (eg breathing only) and the patient needs other expertise (insomnia, parasomnia, paediatric NIV), the patient is referred to a clinician with the relevant expertise. Communication is sent to the original referrer.

5. Records

Subcontractor register; FRM-018; referral correspondence; QA evidence.

6. References

ASA Standard cl. 4.5.1 - 4.5.5.
