  1. FRAUD, WASTE, AND ABUSE POLICY

1. Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to establish guidelines and procedures to prevent, detect, and address fraud, waste, and abuse.

2. Policy

It is the policy to act in compliance with all applicable federal, state, local law and regulations to control and prevent fraud, waste, and abuse (FWA). Preventing, detecting and reporting fraud, waste, and abuse is the responsibility of all employees and management.

Definitions:

Fraud: Involves intentional deception or misrepresentation with the purpose of gaining an unauthorized benefit.

Waste: Unnecessary practices, overuse of resources or inefficient actions that result in unnecessary costs.

Abuse: Actions that go against acceptable health care and billing practices.

3. Procedure

All durable medical equipment (DME) supplier employees will be informed and trained annually on the policies and expectations that follow federal, state, and local laws regarding control and prevention of fraud, waste, and abuse.

The supplier and its employees will not knowingly:

falsify patient information to submit fraudulent claims

bill for services or equipment that were not provided

bill for services that do not support medical necessity

order excessive quantities of medical supplies beyond what is necessary for proper use

bill for services or equipment that do not meet professionally recognized standards

upcode or unbundle services or equipment/supplies

At least annually, the FWA policies will be reviewed to keep current with changing regulations and industry standards.

If any employee identifies or suspects actions of FWA, they can anonymously report their concerns without fear of retaliation to (insert title of leadership) .

Any employee who knowingly engages in any actions construed as fraudulent will be susceptible to disciplinary actions upon investigation and reported to the proper authorities if warranted.
