1. Purpose ASA 4.1.5(b)(c)(d)(e)

To ensure staff are free from undue commercial, financial or other pressures, declare conflicts of interest, and protect patient confidentiality.

2. Conflict of Interest

  1. All staff complete FRM-015 on appointment and annually thereafter, and within 14 days of any new interest.
  2. Declared interests are recorded in the COI register and reviewed by the Medical Director.
  3. Material conflicts are managed (recusal, declaration, divestment); where a service profits from both diagnosis and treatment devices (cl. 5.7.1.10 Note 2) the arrangement is documented and managed at arm's length.

3. Confidentiality

  1. All staff sign a confidentiality agreement at induction.
  2. Hard-copy records stored in locked cabinets; electronic records in role-based encrypted systems.
  3. Result transmission complies with SOP-RPT-003 (secure transmission).
  4. Disclosure to third parties requires patient consent or legal compulsion.

4. Breach Management

Suspected breaches reported to the Medical Director within 24 hours; investigated per SOP-QMS-008; notifiable data breaches reported to the OAIC per Privacy Act.

5. References

ASA Standard cl. 4.1.5; Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and equivalent state/NZ legislation.
