1. Purpose ASA 4.4, 5.4

To ensure referrals are appropriate, triaged for urgency, and scheduled in line with capacity and clinical need.

2. Procedure

  1. Referral received via fax, secure email, EMR or web; logged on FRM-001 within 1 working day.
  2. Administrative completeness check: 3 patient identifiers, clinical details, study type, referrer details, Medicare/insurance details.
  3. Sleep physician triages clinically; confirms appropriateness of the requested test; documents any deviation.
  4. Paediatric referrals routed to paediatric sleep physician.
  5. Urgency category applied:
    • Category 1 (Urgent): safety-critical occupations with suspected significant OSA, or significant comorbidities (unstable IHD, heart failure, hypercapnic respiratory failure, pulmonary hypertension, neuromuscular disease with suspected hypoventilation) - schedule within 4 weeks.
    • Category 2 (Routine): next available within service target.
    • Category 3 (Deferred): patient preparation required before booking.
  6. Booking confirmed with patient; pre-study information pack issued.

3. Demand Management

Where demand exceeds capacity, Tech Lead and Medical Director apply escalation protocol: extended hours, weekend lists, subcontracting per SOP-QMS-011.

4. Records

FRM-001; triage decision log; capacity reports.

5. References

ASA Standard cl. 4.4.1 - 4.4.3, 5.4.1 - 5.4.5.
