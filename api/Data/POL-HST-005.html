1. Purpose

To minimize or eliminate the transmission and spread of infection to ensure the safety of staff and patients. To educate employees about a system of engineering and work practice controls designed to reduce the risk of transmission of microorganisms/pathogens.

2. Policy

The site shall establish a system of engineering and work practice controls designed to reduce the risk of organization-acquired or community-acquired infections and/or communicable diseases.

Universal precautions: An approach to infection control to treat all human blood and certain human body fluids as if they were known to be infectious for HIV, HBV and other blood-borne pathogens.

Blood-borne pathogens standards: An approach to protect staff that may have been exposed to pathogenic microorganisms present in blood through skin, eye, mucous membrane or through needle stick resultant from their duties or responsibilities.

3. Procedure

All staff are required to wash their hands thoroughly:

before and after patient care/service

after using toilet facilities

after removing/handling contaminated equipment

Personal protective equipment (PPE) will be made available to all staff.

PPE provided may include but not be limited to the following:

gloves

can be used during the hook up and removal of sensors and/or contact with a patient

worn if patients with respiratory infection arrive for a study and are not rescheduled

worn when touching body fluids, secretions, and other contaminated objects

protective body clothing (if applicable)

eye protection (eyeglasses with shields, goggles, or face shield)

respiratory protection devices

Disposable masks with current OSHA rating are to be used for protection from inhalation of infectious material (e.g., disposable surgical masks).

CPR shields are used to provide a barrier while providing emergency CPR.

All PPE should be removed (as soon as possible) when they are soiled with infectious materials and appropriately discarded.

Equipment will be cleaned as per the manufacturer’s guidelines (reference Equipment Maintenance policy).

If a needle stick occurs or blood or other bodily fluids come into contact with skin or eyes, immediately report the incident to your supervisor and seek medical attention.
