1. Purpose ASA 5.5.1.1, 5.2.1

To ensure prompt, safe and consistent response to medical and non-medical emergencies during sleep studies.

2. Medical Emergency

  1. Recognise: collapse, severe desaturation, suspected MI, severe arrhythmia, seizure, severe respiratory distress, anaphylaxis.
  2. Activate emergency response: call MET / 000 / hospital emergency code per site policy.
  3. Begin BLS; use AED where available.
  4. Bring oxygen and suction to bedside.
  5. Senior medical staff (on-call) notified.
  6. Document on patient file and incident report; notify referring doctor.

3. Paediatric Emergency

Paediatric BLS by paediatric-trained staff; age-appropriate AED; documented paediatric emergency protocol displayed at bedside.

4. Non-Medical Emergency

Fire, power failure, security: evacuation route signs; emergency lighting; UPS for monitoring; documented fire response per site policy.

5. Drills

Annual emergency drill; outcomes reviewed; learning fed into training.

6. Records

Incident reports; drill records; staff BLS recertification (FRM-014).

7. References

ASA Standard cl. 5.5.1.1, 5.2.1; site/hospital emergency policies.
