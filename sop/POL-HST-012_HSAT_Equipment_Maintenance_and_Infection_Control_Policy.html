1. Purpose

To ensure all HSAT patient-related equipment is monitored for safety, cleaned after every use, and maintained per AASM Standard H-6.

2. Policy

A written plan governs monitoring of all HSAT equipment for electrical and mechanical safety. All equipment is cleaned and inspected after each patient use in accordance with manufacturer recommendations, regulatory standards, and institutional policy.

3. Procedure

Maintenance Plan

  1. Written plan addresses monitoring of all HSAT equipment for electrical and mechanical safety.
  2. Equipment maintenance is documented in a log.
  3. All devices/sensors associated with a failed HSAT (no data or corrupt data) are tested before next use.
  4. Reported or detected failures are categorised, analysed for cause, and a preventive plan documented.

Infection Control

  1. Written infection control procedure covers cleaning and inspection of all patient-related equipment after each use, consistent with manufacturer recommendations and applicable regulations.
  2. Clean and dirty devices are physically separated in compliance with the infection control plan.
  3. Storage areas for clean and dirty equipment are clearly labelled.

Packing, Shipping, and Storage

  1. Written instructions for HSAT and sensor packing, shipping, and storage are maintained.
  2. Equipment is inspected before packaging for patient use.

Unique Device Identification

  1. All reusable equipment has a unique identifier assigned and tracked.
  2. Identifiers are recorded and used for failure investigation.
  3. A device tracking log is maintained and reviewed regularly.

PHI and Physiologic Data

  1. Documented procedure for deleting all PHI and physiologic data from HSAT devices after each use.
  2. Deletion is verified and documented for each device.
