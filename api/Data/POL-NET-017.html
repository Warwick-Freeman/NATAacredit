⚠ CATEGORY I STANDARD — Non-compliance is immediate cause for denial or revocation of accreditation.

1. Purpose

To ensure compliance with all applicable HIPAA and HITECH rules and regulations regarding the confidentiality and protection of Protected Health Information (PHI), in accordance with AASM Standard N-17 (Category I).

2. Policy

The entity abides by all current HIPAA and HITECH rules and regulations. All personnel protect the confidentiality of PHI. Patients have the rights listed below. Compliance is a Category I requirement.

3. Procedure

Protection of PHI

  1. All personnel are responsible for protecting PHI.
  2. Except as permitted by law, personnel shall not share any PHI with any party, including other health care providers, DME companies, employers, or payers.
  3. All employees attest to awareness of federal and state privacy laws; attestation is documented on file.

Patient Rights Regarding PHI

  1. Right to review a privacy notice explaining how PHI will be used and disclosed.
  2. Right to request restrictions on use and disclosure of PHI (the entity is not required to agree).
  3. Right to inspect, copy, and amend their medical records.
  4. Right to an accounting of PHI disclosures.
  5. A current Notice of Privacy Practices is provided to each patient and posted in a clearly visible location at each service location.

Staff Training

  1. The Site Director ensures all appropriate personnel are trained on HIPAA regulations.
  2. New employees are trained within 30 days of hire; refresher training is provided annually or when significant regulatory changes occur.
  3. Proof of training is maintained by the entity.

Breach Notification

  1. The entity maintains written privacy breach notification policies and procedures.
  2. If there is more than a low probability that PHI has been compromised, notification is made in accordance with applicable law.
  3. The Site Director promptly notifies all appropriate parties (e.g., compliance officer, attorney) of any HIPAA violations.
  4. All breaches and notification activities are documented.
