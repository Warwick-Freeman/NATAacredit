1. Purpose

To ensure a safe environment for all staff and patients, effective communication and safe handling practices for hazardous materials will be provided, promoting safety for those who encounter such materials.

2. Policy

Specific procedures for the use, storage and cleanup of any hazardous materials will be implemented to ensure the safety of patients and staff.

3. Procedure

  1. Safety data sheets (SDS) will be maintained for all hazardous materials used and maintained in ____ (indicate location) _______. The information contained in these sheets will be used to respond to a spill or splash.
  2. The site will provide safety education and training on all chemicals used during orientation and as new chemicals are adopted for use.
  3. All chemical substances should remain in their original containers appropriately labeled as hazardous material. Substances that may be transferred to another container must be appropriately labeled with a hazardous material label clearly visible.
  4. All staff must be able to identify all chemicals that may be hazardous by:
    1. reading the label
    2. reviewing the information found on the SDS
  5. All incidents resulting from the use of hazardous materials are to be reported to the immediate supervisor immediately with the completion of an unusual occurrence report within 24 hours of the related occurrence.
  6. Method of cleaning chemical spill or splash:
    1. Isolate the spill.
    2. Secure the area and remove patients.
    3. 6.3 Identify the material/chemical spilled.
    4. Refer to the SDS for proper precautions, handling, cleaning and disposal procedures.
    5. Clean the spill/splash accordingly.
    6. Report the spill/splash.
    7. 6.6 Dispose of material according to OSHA guidelines.
    8. Complete all appropriate documentation forms: an unusual occurrence report.
  7. The following is a list of potential hazardous materials that may be used: (List may be site specific)
    1. acetone
    2. collodion
    3. disinfecting solutions
    4. 8.4 collodion remover
    5. alcohol
    6. bleach
    7. “Wite-Out” (and other text correction fluids)
    8. Cidex (and other disinfecting solutions)
    9. Sani-Wipes (and other sanitizing wipes)
