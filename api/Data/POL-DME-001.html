  1. DME PATIENT ACCEPTANCE, ORDERING, AND EQUIPMENT

1. Purpose

To outline the procedures of admitting patients upon receipt of a prescription order from the ordering physician.

2. Policy

It is the policy to provide care/services upon receipt of a prescription/order from a licensed physician based on medical necessity and equipment availability.

3. Procedure

Acceptance of patients is based upon an expectation that the service provider can safely and adequately meet the patient’s need.

The patient must be under the care of a physician who will supervise ongoing care.

Acceptance is contingent upon medical necessity and availability of medical equipment without regard to age limitation.

All patients are accepted in compliance with all local, federal, state law and regulation, and in adherence to the AASM clinical guideline.

The supplier determines that the medical equipment requested is available and can be delivered in a timely manner.

The supplier obtains all essential patient identifying information:

patients full name

date of birth

contact/demographic information

insurance details

all relevant medical information

Verification of insurance coverage will be conducted to determine eligibility for prescribed equipment.

A financial/payment plan will be developed in conjunction with the patient and insurance provider.

All necessary medical documentation will be obtained as required by the insurance provider for coverage and reimbursement of the specific equipment.

The physician’s office may be contacted to request:

clinical notes

sleep study results to support medical necessity

Physician license and expiration date are verified and documented through the state department or hospital credentialling department.

The ordering physician will be contacted as needed to confirm prescriptions or recommend any necessary changes, refinements, and/or additional evaluations to the prescribed equipment, item(s) and/or services.

A prescription order must include appropriate diagnosis code consistent to the medical equipment ordered.

Dependent upon the type of equipment ordered, a certificate of medical necessity will also be obtained and maintained in the medical record.

All interactions with the health care provider will be documented in the medical record.

Any changes in the patient’s medical equipment will be required to obtain an updated prescription/order which will be kept unaltered in the medical record.

Any time there is a delay in delivery of the equipment/item/service, the ordering physician will be notified.

Periodic updates regarding treatment or the provision of service that may alter the plan of care will be provided to the ordering physician:

patient noncompliance to therapy
