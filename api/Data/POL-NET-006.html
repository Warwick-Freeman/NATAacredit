1. Purpose

Patients being evaluated in a sleep environment, particularly those with obstructive sleep apnea or pulmonary disease, are at risk for catastrophic cardiopulmonary events. To ensure the safety of patients and staff, each service location must be able to manage emergency events and have available appropriate medical equipment.

2. Policy

All staff with be familiar with the location of emergency equipment, (e.g., barrier mask for resuscitation, automated external defibrillator (AED)) and will be required to participate in an annual cardiopulmonary drill. Sleep personnel responsible for patient care will maintain a current certificate in basic life support, including cognitive and manikin skills training.

Written emergency procedures will be maintained to assist the staff in recognizing situations that would require activation of an emergency response. Emergency response procedures are accessible in the In-Lab Emergency Plan, which will include procedures for each of the following:

cardiopulmonary emergencies with requisite equipment

neurologic emergencies, particularly seizures and strokes

psychiatric emergencies, particularly suicidal ideation

environmental emergencies including fire, weather, bomb threat, and belligerent patients
