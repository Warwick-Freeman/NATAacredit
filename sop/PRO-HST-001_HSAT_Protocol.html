1. Purpose

To ensure that the home sleep apnea test (HSAT) adheres to the latest AASM practice guidelines for diagnosing OSA in adults.

2. Policy

HSAT is a method of recording certain parameters that target sleep, cardiovascular, oximetry, position, effort and respiratory measures for the purpose of evaluating patients for uncomplicated, moderate to severe OSA.

HSAT will be performed in conjunction with a comprehensive sleep evaluation by an appropriately licensed sleep medical staff member. All portable monitoring equipment will be FDA-approved and appropriately maintained to ensure patient safety and efficiency of the test.

3. Procedure

An order from an appropriately licensed health care professional must be provided along with a relevant medical history documenting the indication for HSAT that complies with the AASM practice parameters.

For direct referrals, follow the direct referral protocol. All tests will be performed, and records will be maintained consistent with HIPAA regulations.

Sleep staff will contact patient to schedule the HSAT pickup and setup. The appointment date and time will be documented.

HSAT devices will be inspected and prepared for application as per the manufacturer’s recommendations, and infection control plan. Equipment must be inspected to ensure equipment is properly cleaned, free of damage, and any private patient health care information of previous patients has been removed.

The HSAT device will allow display of raw data for manual scoring and editing.

Documentation of the serial number of the device (ID) located on each unit will be recorded in the medical record and on the HSAT equipment the Nexus 360 Equipment Register. The log will include:

date equipment is dispensed

name of patient

type of equipment used and ID number

date equipment is returned

comments: equipment malfunction, test completed, device failures etc.

evidence of routine inspection prior to patient receiving the device

At the time of pickup, the patient will receive HSAT instructions from a trained HSAT staff member on the following:

the use of the device

application and hook up procedure

how to turn the device on/off

preparation of the device for return

other tasks as required

Patients will be given the telephone number for access to technical/professional staff support for troubleshooting problems encountered during HSAT.

Phone number for access during testing hours:

Patients will be instructed to call 911 in case of medical emergency encountered during HSAT.

Sleep staff will arrange the delivery of HSAT equipment to patients when needed. The device will be packaged with all necessary sensors and equipment and shipped using a service that has the capability of tracking the device.

Extra batteries and nasal cannulas will be provided.

Written instructions with pictures and instructional video will be included in the kit provided.

Patients will be called to review the instructions and answer any questions.

Patients will be instructed to discard all disposable items prior to return.

One night will be recorded unless otherwise specified by the ordering provider.

After completion of the HSAT, patients are instructed to return the equipment in the package provided and return it to the sleep facility in person or by mail.

Special instructions for return: packing, mailing, shipping: _________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________.

When the device is returned, all data will be downloaded from the device.

All data will be downloaded and scored within 24 hours of return.

All physiological and PHI data will be erased.

Batteries are removed and discarded.

The device will be taken to a designated dirty area for cleaning.

All nonreusable (disposable) equipment will be discarded in appropriate trash receptacle.

All reusable equipment, including sensors, will be cleaned using germicidal wipes.

Units will be recharged and stored in the designated clean area.

Units will be visually inspected for any apparent signs of damage.

Scoring is performed by trained scoring staff, which may include RST, RPSGT, CPSGT, RRT-SDS OR CRT-SDS.

A person who holds board certification in sleep medicine is required to either conduct the interpretation of the sleep study or review the sleep study interpretation.

The HSAT report will include the “RECOMMENDED” and/or “ACCEPTED” items listed in the most current version of the AASM Scoring Manual and an indication of whether the results support the diagnosis of obstructive sleep apnea.

Recommendations for treatment will be consistent with all applicable AASM practice parameters and clinical practice guidelines.

If the HSAT study did not confirm the diagnosis of OSA, the referring physician will be notified.

All devices and sensors associated with malfunction or failed tests (e.g., no data, or corrupt data) must be reported to the (insert title) _______________ documented on the HSAT equipment the Nexus 360 Equipment Register, taken out of service, sent for repair or replacement. If the equipment is returned, it must be tested for proper function prior to next use.

Reported or detected failures must be categorized and analyzed for cause with a documented plan developed to prevent future failures.
