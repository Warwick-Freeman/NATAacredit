Subcontracting Policy — In-Lab Testing

Standard: AASM L-25, L-26

1. Purpose

To define requirements and oversight obligations when subcontracting HSAT services or sleep study scoring from the In-Lab Testing location.

2. Policy

Any subcontracting of HSAT or scoring services must be governed by a written agreement defining expectations, AASM standard compliance, and annual performance review obligations. Diagnosis may never be subcontracted.

3. Procedure

Subcontracting HSAT (L-25)

  1. The entity may subcontract HSAT; the subcontract must NOT include diagnosis.
  2. A written agreement must: identify specific expectations, require the subcontractor to meet all applicable AASM HSAT Standards, and exclude diagnostic responsibilities.
  3. The entity assesses subcontractor performance against contractual obligations annually.

Subcontracting Scoring (L-26)

  1. A written agreement enumerates performance expectations for scoring subcontractors.
  2. Subcontractor scorers must meet all applicable AASM scoring personnel standards (N-13).
  3. Annual performance assessment is documented and retained ≥5 years.

Subcontractor Review

  1. Annual review assesses compliance with contractual terms and AASM standards.
  2. Identified deficiencies are communicated with a corrective action plan.
  3. Subcontractors unable to meet requirements are replaced.
