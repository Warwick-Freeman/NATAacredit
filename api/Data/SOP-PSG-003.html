1. Purpose ASA 5.5.3

To define how attended PSGs are conducted to ensure recording quality consistent with AASM and ASA standards.

ℹ️ Nexus 360 Integration: Steps in this procedure that are performed or supported by the Nexus 360 quality management system are identified with [Nexus 360] throughout this document. Staff must access Nexus 360 at http://localhost:5173 to complete these steps. The system audit trail satisfies record-keeping requirements for all digitally recorded activities.

2. Pre-Study

  • 3-ID verification (SOP-PSG-002).
  • Patient interview: medications, recent illness, alcohol/caffeine, sleep history, ESS.
  • Adult: prep time >=45 min; paediatric: 1 - 2 h. Roster ratio <= 1 tech : 3 patients adult / 1 tech : 2 paediatric.
  • Electrode/sensor application per SOP-PSG-005 montage; impedance <5 kΩ EEG/EOG/EMG.
  • Biocalibration per SOP-PSG-005 recorded on FRM-019/biocal sheet; pre-study equipment checks logged in Nexus 360 Equipment module [Nexus 360].

3. Study Conduct

  • Technologist in attendance throughout (short absences covered per ASA Note 2). Continuous live signal monitoring from monitoring room.
  • Patient call system tested. Audio-video recording active (mandatory paediatric).
  • Event annotations made in real time: lights-off, lights-on, position change, body movement, awakenings, technical events.
  • Study duration >=8 h (target) - nap studies only as per ASA Note for <12 months.

4. Post-Study

  • End biocal performed and recorded.
  • Sensors removed; patient debriefed; transportation considered for sleepy patients.
  • Adult analysis time >=2 h; paediatric >=4 h.

5. Quality Control

Random review of 10% of studies by Tech Lead; trends shared with technical team and tracked in Nexus 360 Quality Indicators [Nexus 360]. Inter-observer concordance per SOP-QC-001. Any equipment issues or NCs arising during studies are logged in Nexus 360 NC & CAPA module [Nexus 360]. Study register maintained in Nexus 360 Studies & Reports module [Nexus 360].

6. References

ASA Standard cl. 5.5.3.1, 5.5.3.2, 4.1.5(g); AASM Manual current version; ASA Guidelines for Sleep Studies in Adults; ASA Paediatric Clinical Practice Guidelines.
