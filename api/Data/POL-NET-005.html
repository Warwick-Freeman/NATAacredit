1. Purpose

Having an established location for all medical records, or a method of tracking the location of medical records when stored in multiple locations, ensures retrieval of all records when needed. Consistency in filing medical records ensures information is readily available and easily found.

2. Policy

It is our policy that all patients evaluated and treated by the sleep medical staff and those directly referred will have an individual medical record in either written or electronic format. All records will be maintained in an organized and consistent manner.

3. Procedure

  1. Use of an electronic medical record.
    1. A medical record will be created in the electronic medical record (EMR) system for all patients who undergo any sleep related services. This will not be limited only to those requiring a sleep study.
      1. All medical records stored in the EMR will be compliant with HIPAA confidentiality and security rules and will be password-protected.
    2. Only those employees appropriately screened and granted authority will have access to the EMR system.
    3. The administrative support staff is tasked with organizing and maintaining the contents of the medical record as directed by the manager.
      1. All patient-related health and other information as indicated by the system required for medical or financial reasons will be appropriately entered into the EMR system.
      2. demographic information
      3. financial and billing information
      4. referral form to include all demographic and financial/billing information
      5. authorization and consent for services
      6. physician order forms
      7. history and physical evaluations
      8. medications record
      9. patient questionnaires and screening assessments
      10. all consultations with facility director/medical staff members
      11. all documentation made by technologists
      12. all sleep studies and interpretation notes
      13. signed indication by authorized and appropriately trained staff member of approval for study if patient directly referred
    4. Patient medical records will have an automatic computer system, backed up daily with files stored at an offsite location.

Use of a paper medical record

  1. All patients who will have any sleep-related services, including those who did not have a sleep study, will have a paper medical record maintained.
  2. All medical records will be kept in locked file cabinets maintained in the control/technical room.
  3. The contents of the medical record will include but not be limited to:
    1. demographic information
    2. financial and billing information
    3. referral form to include all demographic and financial/billing information
    4. authorization and consent for services
    5. physician order forms
    6. history and physical evaluations
    7. medications record
    8. patient questionnaires and screening assessments
    9. all consultations with facility director/medical staff members
    10. all documentation done by technologists
    11. all sleep studies and interpretation notes
    12. signed indication by authorized and appropriately trained staff member of approval for study if patient directly referred
  4. Only those individuals who are authorized by the manager (e.g., technologists, billing personnel, and administrative staff) will have access to the medical record.
