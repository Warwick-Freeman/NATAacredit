1. Purpose ASA 4.14

To verify that pre-study, study and post-study processes conform to the QMS and the ASA Standard, are implemented and effective, and to drive continual improvement.

2. Audit Cycle

A rolling 12-month audit programme covers every QMS element at least once per cycle. The annual plan is approved by the Medical Director and reviewed at management review.

3. Auditor Competence & Independence

Auditors are trained in QMS auditing and the ASA Standard. Wherever resources permit, auditors are independent of the area audited. Selection is documented in the audit plan.

4. Audit Procedure

  1. Quality Manager publishes annual audit plan with scope, criteria and dates.
  2. Auditor confirms scope and notifies area owner at least 5 working days before audit.
  3. Auditor reviews documents, observes activities, interviews staff, and samples records against criteria.
  4. Findings recorded on FRM-009 (Conformance / Observation / Minor NC / Major NC) with objective evidence.
  5. Closing meeting with area owner.
  6. Audit report issued within 10 working days; nonconformities raised in the NC register.
  7. Area owner agrees corrective actions and target dates.
  8. Auditor verifies effectiveness at next cycle.

5. Outputs to Management Review

Audit summary, trend analysis, status of corrective actions and audit-plan completion are tabled at every management review.

6. Records

Audit plan; FRM-009 reports; NC/CAPA register; verification of corrective action.

7. References

ASA Standard cl. 4.14.1 - 4.14.11.
