1. Purpose

To protect patients and staff from injury and harm, a root cause analysis will be conducted of all significant adverse events.

2. Policy

The network will develop an investigative analysis process that evaluates, identifies trends and resolves issues as necessary for adverse events or incidents that may be of risk to patients and staff members.

3. Definitions

Adverse Event: An adverse event is any incident that may cause harm or injury to a patient and/or staff.

Root Cause Analysis (RCA): RCA is the process of discovering the root causes of problems in order to identify appropriate solutions.

Risk Management: Risk management is the continuing process of identifying, analyzing, evaluating and treating loss exposures and monitoring risk and financial resources to mitigate the adverse effects of loss resulting in liability judgments.

4. Procedure

Each service will document and track significant adverse events or incidents that occur during the hours of operation that minimally include the following:

patient or staff death

permanent loss of function or of a body part by a patient or staff

an event that leads to the hospitalization of a patient or staff

an event that requires activation of an emergency medical response

sexual or physical assault of a patient or staff or allegations thereof

release of a minor or a patient lacking capacity or competency to an unauthorized individual

patient leaves without notifying or against the advice of medical staff (elopement of a patient)

complications arising from the effects of hypnotics used for the purpose of sleep testing

any event required by the applicable jurisdiction to be reported to a government agency

The completed report will be routed to the network director for review and analysis (See Significant Adverse Event Reporting).

The network director will review the description of the specific adverse event to understand what has occurred.

Who was involved in the situation?

When and where did it happen?

What were the contributing factors, situations, circumstances, or conditions that may have caused the event?

Through assessment and analysis of the data, the network director will identify the process and/or root issues that caused the event.

Any event that may pose a potential liability or safety risk will be forwarded to a legal authority if warranted.

A corrective plan of action to mitigate future occurrences will be developed by the network director and implemented by each service. The plan may involve:

changes in workflow/communication processes

in-service education or conferencing

follow-up response to appropriate individuals

A designated time point will be determined to measure the success of the plan.
