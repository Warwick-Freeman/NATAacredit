1. Purpose

The review of a history and physical by either the medical staff member or appropriately trained designee along with documented approval of a sleep study ensures that appropriate testing is performed on all patients who are referred for only a sleep study.

2. Policy

All patients referred for sleep studies must have a current history and physical reviewed and signed by the medical staff member or appropriate designee. This will be kept in the medical record.

Definition

Direct Referrals: Directly referred patients are defined as those not seen in consultation by a sleep medical staff member prior to or within 3 months following an in-lab sleep study or an HSAT originally ordered by a referring physician.

3. Procedure

A physician’s order will be obtained for those patients directly referred (only being referred for testing; not scheduled or expected to be seen by a clinic medical staff member before or after the study).

Orders may be verbally obtained, by fax, mail, or submitted electronically.

A comprehensive history and physical relating to sleep complaints must be submitted.

If the physician is ordering the study for other reasons, the history and physical must state why sleep testing is indicated.

A complete list of current medications, including those for any allergies, will be obtained.

Other medical conditions of the patient will also be obtained.

A clinic medical staff member or appropriately trained technical or administrative staff member will review and approve the information provided and determine if the requested study is indicated.

If there is insufficient information to support the need for the study, the referring provider will be contacted either to obtain further information or notify the physician that the patient does not meet the need for a sleep study. At that time, the patient may be scheduled for an evaluation by a medical staff member.

A copy of all notes and communication with the referring provider will be maintained in the medical record.
