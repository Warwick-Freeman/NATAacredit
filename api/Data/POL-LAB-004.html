1. Purpose

To ensure the safety of patients and personnel and ensure accurate and uninterrupted operation of all patient-related mechanical and electric equipment through routine cleaning and periodic inspections.

2. Policy

All patient-related equipment will be cleaned by staff on a regular basis and routinely monitored and inspected for electrical and mechanical safety consistent with manufacturer’s recommendations and OSHA regulations.

Patient-related equipment includes all owned, borrowed, leased, and consigned equipment used for demonstration purposes and data collection, including oxygen equipment, sensors, bands, oximeters, thermistors, beds, PAP equipment, and biophysiological equipment, computers and equipment located in the control room.

3. Procedure

All equipment used will be inventoried and logged prior to its initial use.

Staff will perform visual, safety and operational tests on all patient-related equipment at the beginning of each shift/prior to each use to identify apparent defects according to manufacturer’s recommendations.

Annual electrical safety testing will be completed by a certified electrician or biomedical engineer.

A record of all equipment inspections will be maintained, updated and documented in the the Nexus 360 Equipment Register.

All in-lab and patient equipment will be inspected as follows:

All units and cables, leads, etc., are inspected nightly by the recording technician and weekly by the manager.

All sensors are inspected nightly by the recording technician and monthly by the manager.

All devices, disposable materials and batteries are inspected prior to shipping or release to patient by the technician.

If any problems are encountered with patient-related equipment, it will be removed from service and will be reported to an authorized individual.

Electrician or biomedical maintenance department will be contacted for inspection, repair or replacement of defective equipment.

Reported or detected failures of devices, sensors and processes will be documented on the the Nexus 360 Equipment Register, and will be categorized and analyzed for cause to prevent future failures.

All disposable equipment will be disposed of in the proper container.

All patient-related nondisposable equipment will be cleaned according to the manufacturer’s recommendations after each use and treated with appropriate disinfectant or germicidal agent if warranted. Clean and dirty equipment will be physically separated in compliance with infection control procedures.
