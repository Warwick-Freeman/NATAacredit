1. Purpose ASA 5.7

To ensure patients and families understand their results and receive evidence-based, well-coordinated follow-up and treatment.

2. Result Communication

  • Result explained by sleep specialist; face-to-face consultation offered and available.
  • Alternative modes (telehealth, phone) acceptable based on clinical circumstances.
  • Family/carer involved in paediatric communication.

3. Treatment Pathways

Documented decision tree (referenced to ASA/AASM best-practice guidelines) covering PAP, oral appliance, upper-airway surgery, positional therapy, weight management, behavioural management, lifestyle advice and sleep hygiene. Where a modality is not offered in-house, referral pathway documented.

4. PAP Provision & Adherence

  1. Diagnostic study completed before PAP prescription.
  2. Early follow-up post-initiation (target 2 - 4 weeks) to manage adherence and side-effects.
  3. Adherence monitored at 30, 90 and 365 days; intervention triggered if average use <4 h/night or <70% nights.
  4. Documented arrangement with external PAP providers where used; feedback loop maintained (SOP-QMS-011).
  5. Quality control program for PAP provision (SOP-QC-001).

5. Provisional CPAP Prescription

Issued on FRM-017 with documented reconciliation to the final report.

6. Specialist Referral

Documented pathways to ENT, dental (oral appliance), facio-maxillary, cardiology, endocrinology, psychiatry, clinical psychology, ICU; complex insomnia management to specialist team or external provider.

7. Diagnostic-Only Services

Where the service provides diagnostics only:

  • Advise referrer on suitability of the diagnostic method.
  • Provide comprehensive report.
  • Offer management advice on request.
  • Implement urgent clinical review (in-house or subcontracted) where severe SDB or acute pathology detected.
  • Make raw data available to referrer on request.

8. References

ASA Standard cl. 5.7.1 - 5.7.2; ASA Best Practice Guidelines; ASA Dental Appliance Therapy 2010; ASA Paediatric Guidelines.
