1. Purpose ASA 4.9

To ensure any aspect of the service's activities that does not conform to the QMS or the agreed requirements of the referring clinician is detected, controlled and addressed.

2. Sources of Nonconformance (NC)

Clinician complaints, QC indications, equipment verifications, consumable checks, staff comments, study reviews, reporting issues, management reviews, internal and external audits, EQA failures, adverse events.

3. Procedure

  1. Any staff member identifying an NC raises FRM-007 within 1 working day.
  2. Quality Manager designates the responsible person.
  3. Clinical significance assessed - where patient safety is or could be affected, the referring clinician is informed and the matter escalated to Medical Director.
  4. Where required, studies are halted and reports withheld.
  5. Immediate (containment) action taken.
  6. Where reports of NC studies have been released, they are recalled or appropriately identified (cl. 4.9.1(f)).
  7. Authorisation to resume normal activity is documented by the designated authority.
  8. Each episode is recorded on FRM-007 and reviewed for trends at monthly QM meeting.

4. Link to CAPA

Every NC is reviewed by the Quality Manager for CAPA requirement per SOP-QMS-009.

5. Records

NC register; FRM-007; trend reports; recall correspondence.

6. References

ASA Standard cl. 4.9.1.
