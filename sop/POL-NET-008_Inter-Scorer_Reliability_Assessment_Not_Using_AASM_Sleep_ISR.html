Inter-Scorer Reliability Assessment — Not Using AASM Sleep ISR
AASM Standards for Accreditation AASM N-24
Document ID: POL-NET-008

Effective date:

Authorised by: Network Director
Standard: AASM N-24

1. Purpose

Regular assessment of scoring reliability for all scoring parameters among all scorers ensures the consistency of scoring in accordance with the current version of the AASM Scoring Manual.

2. Policy

All individuals who score sleep studies will have reliability assessments with the network director, as defined in the AASM Standards for Accreditation, who serves as the gold standard for agreement. Reliability measures are reviewed quarterly by the network director as part of the quality assurance program. All individuals who score sleep studies will independently score the same record.

3. Procedure

  1. A minimum of 200 consecutive 30-second epochs must be compared and assessed in at least three PSGs per quarter for a minimum total of 12 (12 PSGs per year).
  2. The manager/director of scoring staff will randomly select a recording to be scored and will notify the scorers, as well as the network director, of the study to score.
  3. The following parameters are to be compared:
    1. sleep staging epoch by epoch
    2. respiratory events
    3. leg movements
    4. arousals
  4. Scoring is completed by each scorer and the network director. Reliability between each scoring technologist and the network director is compared.
  5. Acceptable values for scorer reliability: overall score of 80% level of agreement
  6. The comparison reports will be kept in a binder and included in the QA reports quarterly
  7. The supervisor/network director will develop an action plan for each technologist when scoring falls below the acceptable level of agreement. Such plans may include, but are not limited to:
    1. review of the current version of the AASM Scoring Manual
    2. additional interscorer assessment with review
    3. focused review/retraining with the supervisor
    4. educational assistance from the facility director
