⚠ CATEGORY I STANDARD — Non-compliance is immediate cause for denial or revocation of accreditation.

1. Purpose

To define the qualifications, appointment, and responsibilities of the Network Director and Site Director in accordance with AASM Standards N-1 through N-4.

2. Policy

The entity must appoint a Network Director and may appoint one or more Site Directors who meet the AASM sleep specialist qualification requirements. The Network Director may also serve as the Site Director. Compliance with N-1 and N-3 is a Category I requirement.

3. Procedure

Network Director Qualifications (N-1 — Category I)

  1. The Network Director must be a sleep specialist who: (a) is a physician or PhD board-certified in sleep medicine by the ABSM, or a physician certified in sleep medicine by a member board of the ABMS or AOA; OR (b) is a physician who has completed an ACGME- or AOA-accredited fellowship in sleep medicine and is eligible to sit for the board exam, with the exam to be passed within three years of fellowship completion.
  2. The entity must appoint one Network Director to oversee services across all locations.
  3. The Network Director may also serve as the Site Director.

Network Director Responsibilities (N-2)

  1. Serves as or designates a primary contact for the AASM and apprises the AASM of any network changes.
  2. Reviews, reports, and manages the quality assurance program on a quarterly basis.
  3. Annually reviews and provides oversight of all network policies and protocols.
  4. Reviews and assesses all safety-related issues, performs risk analysis, and communicates with stakeholders.
  5. Maintains access to EMR information across all locations.
  6. Holds medical licensure in each state covered by the network.
  7. Spends a minimum of 4 hours per month (or 2 hours per location in larger networks) fulfilling these responsibilities via physical presence, conference calls, virtual meetings, or webinars.

Site Director Qualifications (N-3 — Category I)

  1. The Site Director must meet the same sleep-specialist qualifications as the Network Director (see above).
  2. The entity may appoint a single Site Director for all locations, or each location may appoint its own Site Director.

Site Director Responsibilities (N-4)

  1. Ensures only licensed health care professionals with prescriptive authority in the applicable state can request testing.
  2. Responsible for the qualifications and training of all medical, technical, and administrative personnel.
  3. Supervises and provides oversight of all staff.
  4. Assures staff compliance with the AMA Code of Medical Ethics and institutional ethics requirements.
  5. Provides oversight of proper operation and calibration of required equipment.
  6. Spends a minimum of 4 hours per month fulfilling these responsibilities.
