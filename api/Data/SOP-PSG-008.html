1. Purpose ASA 5.1, 5.2.1(a)v/xiii, 5.3.1(e), 5.5, 5.7.1.5

To deliver paediatric sleep studies in a child-safe environment with paediatric-trained staff and equipment.

2. Patient Evaluation

Every paediatric patient is evaluated by a paediatric sleep physician before the study, including assessment of whether the proposed environment is safe and appropriate for the child.

3. Environment

  • Child-safe, age-appropriate bedroom and bedding.
  • Parent sleep accommodation in the child's bedroom available.
  • Age-appropriate sensors and a range of sizes available.
  • Age-appropriate resuscitation equipment including AED, oxygen and suction at the bedside.
  • Paediatric-BLS-trained staff on-site for the duration of every study.

4. Recording & Scoring

Audio-video recording mandatory. Scoring per AASM Manual using paediatric rules and the ASA/ASTA Addendum. Computerised analysis is not recommended for paediatric studies; manual scoring is required.

5. Paediatric NIV / IV Ventilation

Clear protocols for: candidate selection; interface fitting; titration approach; monitoring (SpO2, TcCO2); escalation; ongoing review. NIV/IV provided only by services with documented competence and on-call medical support. Paediatric ENT and facio-maxillary referral pathways documented.

6. Family Communication

Results and implications explained by paediatric sleep physician to the family with opportunity for questions.

7. References

ASA Standard cl. 5.1, 5.2.1, 5.3.1(e), 5.5, 5.7.1.5, 5.8.5; ASA Paediatric Guidelines 2017; ASTA/ASA Paediatric Addendum.
