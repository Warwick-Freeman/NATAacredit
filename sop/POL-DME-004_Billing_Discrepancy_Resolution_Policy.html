billing discrepancy resolution

1. Purpose

To ensure accuracy and integrity of our organization by identifying and resolving any billing errors or discrepancies made to insurance payer or patients.

2. Policy

It is our policy to proactively, promptly, identify and correct any billing discrepancies that may occur in transactions with patients and or health insurance providers. We believe that accurate billing is essential for a strong financial foundation and positive relationships.

3. Procedure

Prior to Billing

All referrals for DME are reviewed at time of referral for active insurance and coverage determination.

Service or delivery does not occur until DME Benefits are determined and the patient has verbally been informed of any copay /deductible issue.

Patients not able to afford DME costs may be offered a payment plan; or a determination of if financial hardship would be appropriate.

All patients are provided with a letter at setup noting coverage determination and financial responsibility.

The most current CPT/ICD codes are used in the billing process.

Annually all billing/coding staff are trained in the most current, coding procedures.

Billing Department Discovery of Error

The billing department reviews all delivered equipment receipts (delivery ticket) for accuracy, medical necessity, and proper diagnosis codes.

If there is an error discovered during the review of the delivery ticket, the financial manager will be notified, and a determination made as to any adjustments:

Diagnosis coding, quantity, or patient information.

If there is an error discovered after the claim has been filed or paid the following may occur:

If there is an overpayment, notification to the insurance payor regarding the error will be submitted.

If there is an error in equipment, services etc., the initial claim/invoice will be voided, and new invoice filed.

If the patient has overpaid or has been charged for the wrong equipment, invalid services, a refund will be mailed with a written letter.

Any patient that has billing questions or concerns shall be directed to the billing staff who are trained in handling any issues.
