  1. FINANCIAL HARDSHIP/CHARITY POLICY

1. Purpose

To outline the criteria and procedures for offering financial assistance to eligible individuals in need of durable medical equipment (DME).

2. Policy

It is the policy to not waive copayments, deductibles, or other owed amounts that are the patient’s responsibility. It is the policy to offer financial assistance if certain eligibility criteria are met.

  1. Eligibility Criteria:
  2. demonstrated financial hardship, evidenced by (state or federal) income levels and inability to afford necessary DME
  3. provision of required application documents deemed necessary

3. Procedure

Patients seeking assistance with payment must submit a written letter requesting financial assistance and provide the following information:

proof of financial hardship; income statements, tax returns, or other relevant documents

documentation of the need for the requested DME

any other information the patient deems appropriate

The submitted information will be reviewed by (insert name of management staff of supplier)

Approval or denial of assistance will be communicated to the patient in writing; a copy will be maintained in the patient’s billing file.

All information provided by the patient will be treated confidentially. Only authorized representatives will have access to the information.

Financial hardship state and federal poverty levels will be reviewed and updated annually.
